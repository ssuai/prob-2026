# 3.


A fair coin is tossed (n) times independently. Let (X_i) be the result of the (i)-th toss, where (X_i=1) if the outcome is a head and (X_i=0) if the outcome is a tail. Define the sample mean as (S_n=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i). Find the expectation (E[S_n]) and the variance (\mathrm{Var}(S_n)). Then, define a new random variable (Z = nS_n), interpret what (Z) represents, and compute (\mathrm{Var}(Z)). Finally, explain what happens to (S_n) as (n) increases.
