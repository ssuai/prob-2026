# Problem 3: Auto-Battler Damage Engine

**Problem Statement:**
You are analyzing the damage output of a specific unit in an auto-battler simulation. In a given round, the unit's Base Attack Damage ($X$) and its Critical Multiplier ($Y$) are modeled as discrete random variables. Because the unit's abilities scale dynamically based on its placement on the board, $X$ and $Y$ are not independent.

Let $X \in \{10, 20, 30\}$ represent the base damage.
Let $Y \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ represent the multiplier (where $1$ is a normal strike, $2$ is a critical strike, and $3$ is a rare mega-critical strike).

The joint probability mass function $P_{X,Y}(x, y)$ for a single attack is given by the following table:

| | $Y=1$ | $Y=2$ | $Y=3$ |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **$X=10$** | 0.15 | 0.05 | 0.00 |
| **$X=20$** | 0.25 | 0.15 | 0.10 |
| **$X=30$** | 0.10 | 0.10 | 0.10 |

Let $Z$ be the Total Damage dealt by the unit in a single strike, defined as the product of the base damage and the multiplier: 
$$Z = X \cdot Y$$

**Your Task:**
1. Find the Probability Mass Function (PMF) of the total damage, $Z$.
2. Calculate the Expected Total Damage, $E[Z]$.
3. Calculate the Variance of the Total Damage, $var[Z]$.
