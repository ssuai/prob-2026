# Problem 1 (Easy)

A biased coin is tossed 3 times independently. The probability of heads on each toss is $p$ ($0 < p < 1$).

Let $X$ be the number of heads obtained in the three tosses.

Define a new random variable $Y = |X - 1|$.

Task: Find the probability mass function (PMF) of $Y$.

---

# Solution

$X \sim \text{Binomial}(3,p)$, so

$$
P(X=0)=(1-p)^3,\quad P(X=1)=3p(1-p)^2
$$

$$
P(X=2)=3p^2(1-p),\quad P(X=3)=p^3
$$

Since $Y = |X - 1|$,

$$
X=0 \to Y=1,\quad X=1 \to Y=0,\quad X=2 \to Y=1,\quad X=3 \to Y=2
$$

Thus,

$$
P(Y=0)=P(X=1)=3p(1-p)^2
$$

$$
P(Y=1)=P(X=0)+P(X=2)=(1-p)^3+3p^2(1-p)
$$

$$
P(Y=2)=P(X=3)=p^3
$$

Therefore, the PMF of $Y$ is

$$
p_Y(y)=
\begin{cases}
3p(1-p)^2, & y=0 \\
(1-p)^3+3p^2(1-p), & y=1 \\
p^3, & y=2 \\
0, & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
