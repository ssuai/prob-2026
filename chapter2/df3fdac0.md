A communication system transmits messages. Let (Y \in {1,2}) denote the message length, where (P(Y=1)=0.7) and (P(Y=2)=0.3). The conditional probability mass function of the transmission time (X) is given as follows: when (Y=1), (P(X=1 \mid Y=1)=0.6) and (P(X=2 \mid Y=1)=0.4); when (Y=2), (P(X=2 \mid Y=2)=0.5) and (P(X=3 \mid Y=2)=0.5). Find the joint probability mass function (p_{X,Y}(x,y)), then derive the marginal probability mass function (p_X(x)), compute (E[X]), and determine whether (X) and (Y) are independent.
