Problem 1.1: Functions and Variance
Let X be a Poisson random variable representing the number of particles emitted by a radioactive source in a one-minute interval, with an average rate of lambda = 4. The energy output of the sensor observing these particles is governed by the function:
Y = 3X - 2

1.Expected Value: Determine the expected energy output, E[Y].

2.Variance: Calculate the variance of the energy output, Var(Y).
