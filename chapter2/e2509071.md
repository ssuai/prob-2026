## [Level 3: Hard] Conditional Independence and Expectations
Problem: Suppose you have two discrete random variables, $X$ and $Y$. It is known that $X$ and $Y$ are not independent in general. However, they are conditionally independent given a specific event $A$. You are provided with the following information:The conditional expectation of $X$ given $A$ is $E[X|A] = 4$.The conditional expectation of $Y$ given $A$ is $E[Y|A] = 7$.The variance of $X$ given $A$ is $\text{var}(X|A) = 2$.The variance of $Y$ given $A$ is $\text{var}(Y|A) = 5$.Task:Calculate the conditional expectation of their product, $E[XY | A]$.Calculate the conditional variance of their sum, $\text{var}(X + Y | A)$.
IMPORTANT: Do not simply provide the final numerical answer. Please write down the detailed derivation process, including the specific properties of conditional independence and the variance of sums that you are applying to reach your conclusion.
