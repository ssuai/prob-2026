# Probability Mass Function (PMF) Practice Problem: Hard

## Problem: The St. Petersburg Paradox
In a casino game, you flip a fair coin repeatedly until you get a Head. If the first Head appears on the $x$-th flip, your prize payout is $Y = 2^x$ dollars.

* **Task 1:** Find the PMF of your prize $Y$.
* **Task 2:** Briefly explain what happens if you attempt to calculate the Expected Value (average payout) of $Y$.
