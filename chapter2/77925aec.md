Topic: Joint PMFs defined by functions, Marginal Distributions, and Independence

Problem: Consider two discrete random variables $X$ and $Y$. The range of $X$ is $\{1, 2\}$ and the range of $Y$ is $\{1, 2, 3\}$. The joint probability mass function (PMF) $p_{X,Y}(x,y)$ is defined by the following formula: $$p_{X,Y}(x,y) = c(x + y)$$where $c$ is a constant.

Questions:Find the value of the constant $c$ that makes this a valid joint PMF.Determine the marginal PMFs $p_X(x)$ and $p_Y(y)$ for all possible values.Are $X$ and $Y$ independent? Justify your answer by checking if $p_{X,Y}(1,1) = p_X(1)p_Y(1)$.Calculate the conditional expectation $E[Y | X=2]$.
