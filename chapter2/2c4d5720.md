Problem:
In a communication channel, a signal $X$ is transmitted and a signal $Y$ is received. $X$ is a Bernoulli random variable with $P(X=1) = 0.6$ (representing a high signal) and $P(X=0) = 0.4$ (representing a low signal). Due to noise in the data path, the received signal $Y$ is conditioned on $X$ as follows:
If $X=0$, $Y$ follows a uniform distribution over $\{0, 1, 2\}$.
If $X=1$, $Y$ follows a distribution where $P(Y=1|X=1) = 0.7$ and $P(Y=2|X=1) = 0.3$.
1.Construct the complete joint PMF table for $X$ and $Y$.
2.Find the conditional PMF of $X$ given that the received signal is $Y=1$ (i.e., $p_{X|Y}(x|1)$).
3.Determine whether $X$ and $Y$ are independent. Justify your answer using the definition of independence $p_{X,Y}(x,y) = p_X(x)p_Y(y)$.
