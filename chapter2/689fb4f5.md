Let random variable $W$ represent the weather conditions in a zone where an autonomous delivery robot operates. The probability of clear weather ($W=0$) is 0.7, and the probability of heavy rain ($W=1$) is 0.3.The robot attempts a total of $n=5$ deliveries per day in this zone. Let random variable $D$ be the number of successful deliveries. The distribution of $D$ is conditioned on the weather $W$:
On a clear day ($W=0$), each delivery is an independent trial with a success probability of 0.8, making $D$ a binomial random variable.
On a rainy day ($W=1$), each delivery is an independent trial with a success probability of 0.4, making $D$ a binomial random variable.

(a) Calculate the conditional expectations $E[D|W=0]$ and $E[D|W=1]$.
(b) Calculate the expected number of successful deliveries in a day, $E[D]$.
(c) Calculate the total probability $P(D=5)$ that all 5 deliveries are successful in a given day.
