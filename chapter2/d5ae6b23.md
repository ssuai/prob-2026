The Problem: The Dual-Source Power Grid
A small research station is powered by two independent energy sources: a backup generator and a solar array.
The Backup Generator (X): This is a discrete random variable. Due to different fuel-saving modes, its power output X (in kilowatts) has the following Probability Mass Function (PMF):
    pX(0)=0.2 (Off)
    pX(5)=0.3 (Half-power)
    pX(10)=0.5 (Full-power)
The Solar Array (Y): This is a continuous random variable representing hourly output. On a typical day, its output Y is uniformly distributed between 2 kW and 6 kW.
The station manager uses a "Power Index" U to track performance, defined by the linear relationship: 
U=3X+2Y
Calculate the following:
The expected power index, E[U].
The variance of the power index, var(U), assuming X and Y are independent.
