Problem 2.1: Sequential Random Variables (Geometric to Binomial)
An experimental protocol dictates that a researcher repeatedly calibrates a machine until a successful calibration occurs. The probability of a successful calibration on any single attempt is p. Let N be the total number of attempts required. Once the machine is calibrated (taking N attempts), the researcher conducts exactly N independent trials of an experiment, where each trial yields a successful result with probability q. Let X be the total number of successful experimental trials.

1.Distributions: Identify the distribution of N and the conditional distribution of X given N = n.

2.Conditional Expectation: Express the conditional expectation E[X|N] as a function of N.

3.Law of Total Expectation: Using the property of expectation (derived via summations of the joint PMF), find the unconditional expectation E[X].

4.Joint PMF: Determine the joint PMF P(N=n, X=k) for n >= 1 and 0 <= k <= n.
