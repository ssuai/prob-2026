### Problem 2 - Intermediate

A data analyst is categorizing a population into two groups: Group A and Group B. 60% of the population belongs to Group A, and 40% belongs to Group B.
An individual from Group A has a $0.1$ probability of a certain event occurring ($X=1$).
An individual from Group B has a $0.4$ probability of the same event occurring ($X=1$).
If an individual is chosen at random from the entire population, find the expected value $E[X]$ of the event occurring.
