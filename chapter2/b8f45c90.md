# Problem 2 (Intermediate)

A random experiment produces two discrete random variables $X$ and $Y$ with joint PMF

$$
p_{X,Y}(x,y)=c(x+y)
$$

for $x \in \{1,2\}$ and $y \in \{1,2,3\}$, and $p_{X,Y}(x,y)=0$ otherwise.

Task: Find $E[X+Y]$.

---

# Solution

First, normalize the PMF:

$$
\sum_{x=1}^{2}\sum_{y=1}^{3} c(x+y)=1
$$

$$
(1+1)+(1+2)+(1+3)+(2+1)+(2+2)+(2+3)=21
$$

$$
21c=1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad c=\frac{1}{21}
$$

---

Using linearity,

$$
E[X+Y]=E[X]+E[Y]
$$

---

Compute $E[X]$:

$$
p_X(1)=\frac{2+3+4}{21}=\frac{9}{21}=\frac{3}{7}, \quad
p_X(2)=\frac{3+4+5}{21}=\frac{12}{21}=\frac{4}{7}
$$

$$
E[X]=1 \cdot \frac{3}{7} + 2 \cdot \frac{4}{7}=\frac{11}{7}
$$

---

Compute $E[Y]$:

$$
p_Y(1)=\frac{2+3}{21}=\frac{5}{21}, \quad
p_Y(2)=\frac{3+4}{21}=\frac{7}{21}, \quad
p_Y(3)=\frac{4+5}{21}=\frac{9}{21}
$$

$$
E[Y]=1 \cdot \frac{5}{21} + 2 \cdot \frac{7}{21} + 3 \cdot \frac{9}{21}
=\frac{46}{21}
$$

---

Therefore,

$$
E[X+Y]=\frac{11}{7}+\frac{46}{21}
=\frac{33}{21}+\frac{46}{21}
=\frac{79}{21}
$$

$$
\boxed{E[X+Y]=\frac{79}{21}}
$$
