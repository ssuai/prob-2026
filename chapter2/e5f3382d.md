3. Hard: Joint DependenciesTwo discrete random variables, $X$ and $Y$, have a joint probability mass function $p(x, y)$ given by:$$p(x, y) = \frac{x + y}{k}$$for $x \in \{1, 2\}$ and $y \in \{1, 2, 3\}$.
For all other values, $p(x, y) = 0$.

Task A: Determine the value of the constant $k$ that makes this a valid joint PMF.
Task B: Find the marginal probability mass functions $p_X(x)$ and $p_Y(y)$.
Task C: Determine whether $X$ and $Y$ are independent. Provide a mathematical justification for your answer.
Task D: Calculate the conditional expectation $E[Y | X = 2]$.
