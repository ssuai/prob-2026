**Problem Statement: Analysis of a Binomial Random Variable**

A university probability class has $n = 150$ students. Assume that each student has a probability $p = 1/3$ of receiving an grade of "A," and that this outcome is independent for each student. Let the random variable $X$ represent the total number of students in the class who receive an "A".

1. Identify the type of standard discrete distribution that $X$ follows and provide its probability mass function (PMF), $p_X(k)$.
2. Calculate the expected number of students who will receive an "A," denoted by $E[X]$.
3. Calculate the variance of $X$, denoted by $var(X)$.
