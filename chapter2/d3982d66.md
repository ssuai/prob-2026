# Problem 3 — Multiple Random Variables, Independence & Conditioning

## Problem Statement

Let \( X \) and \( Y \) be independent discrete random variables, each uniformly distributed on:

\[
\{1, 2, \dots, n\}
\]

Define:

\[
Z = \gcd(X, Y)
\]

---

## Questions

1. Find an expression for:

\[
P(Z = 1)
\]

2. Show that as \( n \to \infty \):

\[
P(Z = 1) \to \frac{1}{\zeta(2)}
\]

where \( \zeta(\cdot) \) is the Riemann zeta function.

3. Using conditioning on \( Z \), determine whether \( X \) and \( Y \) remain independent given that:

\[
\gcd(X, Y) = 1
\]
