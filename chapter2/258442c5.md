# Problem 3 (Hard)

A biased coin is tossed $n$ times independently, where the probability of heads on each toss is $p$.

Let $X_i$ be the random variable defined as

$$
X_i =
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if the $i$-th toss is a head} \\
0, & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

for $i = 1,2,\dots,n$.

Define the sample mean

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i.
$$

Task: Find $\mathrm{var}(S_n)$.

---

# Solution

Each $X_i$ is a Bernoulli random variable with parameter $p$, so

$$
E[X_i]=p, \qquad \mathrm{var}(X_i)=p(1-p).
$$

Since the tosses are independent, the random variables $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n$ are independent. Therefore,

$$
\mathrm{var}\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i\right)
=
\sum_{i=1}^{n} \mathrm{var}(X_i)
=
np(1-p).
$$

Now,

$$
S_n=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i.
$$

Using the variance rule $\mathrm{var}(aX)=a^2\mathrm{var}(X)$,

$$
\mathrm{var}(S_n)
=
\mathrm{var}\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i\right)
=
\frac{1}{n^2}\mathrm{var}\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i\right).
$$

Substituting the result above,

$$
\mathrm{var}(S_n)
=
\frac{1}{n^2}\cdot np(1-p)
=
\frac{p(1-p)}{n}.
$$

Thus,

$$
\boxed{\mathrm{var}(S_n)=\frac{p(1-p)}{n}}.
$$
