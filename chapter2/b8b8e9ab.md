1. Easy: The Defective BatchA quality control inspector examines a box containing 6 microchips.
Unknown to the inspector, 2 of the chips are defective and 4 are functional.
The inspector randomly selects 3 chips without replacement.
Let $X$ be the random variable representing the number of defective chips found in the sample.

Task A: Construct the probability mass function (PMF) for $X$, denoted as $P(X = x)$ for all possible values of $x$.
Task B: Calculate the probability that the inspector finds at least one defective chip.
