A memory controller is designed to transmit data packets (modeled as an FSM). Each 

transmission attempt takes exactly 1 cycle.



1. With probability 1/3, the transmission succeeds. If successful, it takes 2 additional 
cycles to receive an ACK signal, after which the entire process terminates.
2. With probability 2/3, the transmission fails (collision). In this case, the 1 cycle just 

spent is wasted, the system resets to its initial state, and it must retry the 

transmission from the beginning (possessing the memoryless property where past 

failures do not affect future attempts).



Let T be the total number of cycles consumed until the packet is successfully transmitted, the 

ACK is received, and the process terminates. Find the expected value E\[T] using the Total

Expectation Theorem.
