## Problem

🇰🇷 **Korean**

어느 통신 채널은 한 번의 전송 시도에서 독립적으로 패킷 하나를 성공적으로 전달할 확률이 $p = \frac{2}{3}$이다.
시스템은 전송이 **처음으로 성공**할 때까지 재시도를 반복하며, 최대 $N = 4$번까지만 시도한다.

확률변수 $X$를 **처음으로 성공하는 시도 번호**로 정의한다. 단, 4번 이내에 성공하지 못하면 $X = 5$로 정의한다 (시스템 실패 코드).

실패 확률을 $q = 1 - p = \frac{1}{3}$으로 표기한다.

다음을 구하라.

1. $X$의 확률질량함수(PMF) $p_X(k)$를 $k = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$에 대해 구하고, 합이 1임을 확인하라.
2. $X$의 기댓값 $E[X]$를 정의로부터 직접 계산하라.
3. $X$의 분산 $\mathrm{Var}(X)$를 $\mathrm{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2$ 공식으로 구하라.
4. 전송 비용 함수를 $g(X) = 3X^2 - 2X + 1$로 정의할 때, 기댓값의 법칙(Law of the Unconscious Statistician, §2.3)을 적용하여 $E[g(X)]$를 구하라.
5. $E[g(X)]$와 $3E[X^2] - 2E[X] + 1$이 같음을 보여 기댓값의 선형성을 확인하라.

---

🇺🇸 **English**

A communication channel transmits one packet per attempt, succeeding independently with probability $p = \frac{2}{3}$ on each try.
The system retries until the **first successful transmission**, but makes at most $N = 4$ attempts.

Define the random variable $X$ as the **index of the first successful attempt**. If no success occurs within 4 attempts, set $X = 5$ (a system failure code).

Let $q = 1 - p = \frac{1}{3}$ denote the failure probability per attempt.

Answer the following:

1. Derive the PMF $p_X(k)$ for $k = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$, and verify that the probabilities sum to 1.
2. Compute $E[X]$ directly from the definition of expectation.
3. Compute $\mathrm{Var}(X)$ using the formula $\mathrm{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2$.
4. Define the cost function $g(X) = 3X^2 - 2X + 1$. Apply the Law of the Unconscious Statistician (§2.3) to compute $E[g(X)]$.
5. Verify that $E[g(X)] = 3E[X^2] - 2E[X] + 1$, thereby confirming the linearity of expectation.

---

## Solution

> **[Step 1] Model Setup**
>
> The sample space is defined by the sequence of Bernoulli trials:
> $$\Omega = \{S,\ FS,\ FFS,\ FFFS,\ FFFF\}$$
> where $S$ = success and $F$ = failure on a single attempt.
>
> The random variable $X$ maps each outcome as follows:
>
> | Outcome | $X$ value | Probability |
> |---|---|---|
> | $S$ | 1 | $p$ |
> | $FS$ | 2 | $qp$ |
> | $FFS$ | 3 | $q^2 p$ |
> | $FFFS$ | 4 | $q^3 p$ |
> | $FFFF$ | 5 | $q^4$ |
>
> With $p = \frac{2}{3}$ and $q = \frac{1}{3}$.

---

> **[Step 2] Key Observation / Diagram**
>
> The structure is a **truncated geometric** distribution. The timeline of attempts:
>
> ```
> Attempt:   1       2       3       4       (fail all 4)
>            |       |       |       |            |
>           X=1     X=2     X=3     X=4          X=5
>            p      qp     q²p     q³p           q⁴
>           2/3    2/9    2/27    2/81          1/81
> ```
>
> Key formulas to be applied (Bertsekas §2.3–2.4):
> $$E[X] = \sum_k k\, p_X(k), \quad E[g(X)] = \sum_k g(k)\, p_X(k), \quad \mathrm{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2$$

---

> **[Step 3] Derivation**
>
> ### Part (1) — PMF of $X$
>
> $$p_X(1) = p = \frac{2}{3}$$
> $$p_X(2) = qp = \frac{1}{3}\cdot\frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{9}$$
> $$p_X(3) = q^2 p = \frac{1}{9}\cdot\frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{27}$$
> $$p_X(4) = q^3 p = \frac{1}{27}\cdot\frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{81}$$
> $$p_X(5) = q^4 = \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^4 = \frac{1}{81}$$
>
> **Sum check** (common denominator 81):
> $$\frac{54}{81} + \frac{18}{81} + \frac{6}{81} + \frac{2}{81} + \frac{1}{81} = \frac{81}{81} = 1 \quad \checkmark$$
>
> ---
>
> ### Part (2) — Expectation $E[X]$
>
> $$E[X] = \sum_{k=1}^{5} k\, p_X(k) = 1\cdot\frac{54}{81} + 2\cdot\frac{18}{81} + 3\cdot\frac{6}{81} + 4\cdot\frac{2}{81} + 5\cdot\frac{1}{81}$$
>
> Computing each numerator term:
> $$= \frac{54 + 36 + 18 + 8 + 5}{81} = \frac{121}{81}$$
>
> ---
>
> ### Part (3) — Variance $\mathrm{Var}(X)$
>
> First, compute $E[X^2] = \sum_{k=1}^{5} k^2\, p_X(k)$:
>
> $$E[X^2] = 1^2\cdot\frac{54}{81} + 2^2\cdot\frac{18}{81} + 3^2\cdot\frac{6}{81} + 4^2\cdot\frac{2}{81} + 5^2\cdot\frac{1}{81}$$
> $$= \frac{54 + 72 + 54 + 32 + 25}{81} = \frac{237}{81} = \frac{79}{27}$$
>
> Then:
> $$\mathrm{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 = \frac{237}{81} - \left(\frac{121}{81}\right)^2 = \frac{237}{81} - \frac{14641}{6561}$$
>
> Convert to denominator 6561:
> $$= \frac{237 \times 81}{6561} - \frac{14641}{6561} = \frac{19197 - 14641}{6561} = \frac{4556}{6561}$$
>
> ---
>
> ### Part (4) — $E[g(X)]$ via Law of the Unconscious Statistician
>
> By §2.3, for $g(X) = 3X^2 - 2X + 1$:
> $$E[g(X)] = \sum_{k=1}^{5} g(k)\, p_X(k) = \sum_{k=1}^{5} (3k^2 - 2k + 1)\, p_X(k)$$
>
> Compute $g(k)$ for each $k$:
>
> | $k$ | $g(k) = 3k^2-2k+1$ | $p_X(k)$ | $g(k)\cdot p_X(k)$ (×81) |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | 1 | $3-2+1=2$ | $54/81$ | $108$ |
> | 2 | $12-4+1=9$ | $18/81$ | $162$ |
> | 3 | $27-6+1=22$ | $6/81$ | $132$ |
> | 4 | $48-8+1=41$ | $2/81$ | $82$ |
> | 5 | $75-10+1=66$ | $1/81$ | $66$ |
>
> $$E[g(X)] = \frac{108 + 162 + 132 + 82 + 66}{81} = \frac{550}{81}$$
>
> ---
>
> ### Part (5) — Linearity of Expectation Verification
>
> Using linearity of expectation (Bertsekas §2.4):
> $$3E[X^2] - 2E[X] + 1 = 3\cdot\frac{237}{81} - 2\cdot\frac{121}{81} + 1 = \frac{711 - 242 + 81}{81} = \frac{550}{81}$$
>
> This equals $E[g(X)] = \dfrac{550}{81}$ ✓, confirming linearity. $\blacksquare$

---

> **[Step 4] Computation Summary**
>
> | Quantity | Exact Value | Decimal |
> |---|---|---|
> | $p_X(1)$ | $\dfrac{54}{81}$ | $0.67$ |
> | $p_X(2)$ | $\dfrac{18}{81}$ | $0.22$ |
> | $p_X(3)$ | $\dfrac{6}{81}$ | $0.07$ |
> | $p_X(4)$ | $\dfrac{2}{81}$ | $0.02$ |
> | $p_X(5)$ | $\dfrac{1}{81}$ | $0.01$ |
> | $E[X]$ | $\dfrac{121}{81}$ | $1.49$ |
> | $E[X^2]$ | $\dfrac{237}{81}$ | $2.93$ |
> | $\mathrm{Var}(X)$ | $\dfrac{4556}{6561}$ | $0.69$ |
> | $E[g(X)]$ | $\dfrac{550}{81}$ | $6.79$ |

---

> **[Step 5] Verification**
>
> - All $p_X(k) \geq 0$ and $\sum_k p_X(k) = 1$ ✓
> - $\mathrm{Var}(X) = \dfrac{4556}{6561} > 0$ ✓ (variance is always non-negative)
> - $E[g(X)]$ computed via LOTUS $=$ computed via linearity $= \dfrac{550}{81}$ ✓

**Answers:**

$$E[X] = \frac{121}{81} \approx 1.49, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(X) = \frac{4556}{6561} \approx 0.69$$

$$E[g(X)] = E[3X^2 - 2X + 1] = \frac{550}{81} \approx 6.79$$
