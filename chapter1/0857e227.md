# Topic: Total Probability Theorem and Bayes' Rule

**Problem:**
A medical lab uses a diagnostic test for a rare disease that affects 0.1% of the population (P(D) = 0.001). The test results are characterized as follows:
* For a person with the disease, the test is positive with a probability of 0.95 (Correct detection). 
* For a person without the disease, the test is positive with a probability of 0.05 (False alarm). 

**Questions:**
1. What is the total probability that a randomly selected person tests positive (P(Positive))? 
2. If a person tests positive, what is the posterior probability that they actually have the disease (P(D | Positive))? Explain why this value might be unexpectedly low.
