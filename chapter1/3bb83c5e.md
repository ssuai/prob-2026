**Problem 3:**
A digital communications channel transmits binary digits (0 or 1). Due to signal noise, errors occasionally occur during transmission:
- A transmitted "0" is incorrectly received as a "1" with a 10% probability.
- A transmitted "1" is incorrectly received as a "0" with a 15% probability.

Historical data shows that 60% of all transmitted bits are "0"s, and the rest are "1"s. 

If the receiver processes an incoming signal and reads it as a "1", what is the exact probability that the original bit transmitted was actually a "1"?
