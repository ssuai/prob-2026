## Problem

🇰🇷 **Korean**

Alice는 매주 확률 수업을 수강하며, 각 주가 끝날 때 그녀의 상태는 **"최신(up-to-date)"** 또는 **"뒤처짐(behind)"** 중 하나이다. 전이 확률은 아래와 같다.

- 현재 주에 최신 상태이면, 다음 주에도 최신 상태일 확률은 $0.8$, 뒤처질 확률은 $0.2$이다.
- 현재 주에 뒤처진 상태이면, 다음 주에 최신 상태가 될 확률은 $0.4$, 여전히 뒤처질 확률은 $0.6$이다.

Alice는 수업 시작 시점에 **최신 상태**이다 (즉, $U_0$ = "0주차 이후 최신" 이 확실하게 성립).

$U_n$을 "$n$주 이후 최신 상태"인 사건, $B_n$을 "$n$주 이후 뒤처진 상태"인 사건으로 정의하자.

**(1)** 위 상황을 확률 모델로 명확히 서술하고, 전확률 공식을 반복적으로 적용하기 위한 점화식을 유도하여라.

**(2)** 이 점화식과 초기 조건을 이용하여 $P(U_1)$, $P(U_2)$, $P(U_3)$를 차례로 계산하여라.

**(3)** $n \to \infty$일 때 $P(U_n)$이 수렴하는지 분석하고, 수렴한다면 극한값 $\pi_U = \lim_{n \to \infty} P(U_n)$을 구하여라. 이 극한값이 만족하는 방정식을 전확률 공식으로부터 직접 유도하여라.

**(4)** 만약 Alice가 0주차에 **뒤처진 상태**에서 시작한다면, $P(U_3)$는 어떻게 달라지는가? 계산 결과를 (2)와 비교하고, 초기 조건이 장기적으로 미치는 영향에 대해 논하여라.

---

🇺🇸 **English**

Alice is taking a probability course. At the end of each week, her status is either **up-to-date** or **behind**, according to the following transition probabilities:

- If she is up-to-date in a given week, the probability that she will be up-to-date (respectively, behind) in the next week is $0.8$ (respectively, $0.2$).
- If she is behind in a given week, the probability that she will be up-to-date (respectively, behind) in the next week is $0.4$ (respectively, $0.6$).

Alice is **up-to-date at the start** of the course (i.e., $U_0$ is certain).

Let $U_n$ denote the event that Alice is up-to-date after $n$ weeks, and $B_n$ the event that she is behind after $n$ weeks.

**(1)** Formulate a precise probability model for this situation and derive a recurrence relation for $P(U_n)$ by applying the Total Probability Theorem.

**(2)** Using the recurrence relation and the initial condition, compute $P(U_1)$, $P(U_2)$, and $P(U_3)$ step by step.

**(3)** Analyze whether $P(U_n)$ converges as $n \to \infty$. If it does, determine the limiting value $\pi_U = \lim_{n \to \infty} P(U_n)$ by deriving the equation it must satisfy directly from the Total Probability Theorem.

**(4)** Suppose instead that Alice starts the course **behind** (i.e., $B_0$ is certain). Recompute $P(U_3)$ and compare it with the result of part **(2)**. Discuss the long-run impact of the initial condition.

## Solution

> **[Step 1] Model Setup**
>
> The experiment unfolds week by week. The sample space at each stage consists of two states: **U** (up-to-date) and **B** (behind). The probability law is defined by the transition probabilities.
>
> **Partition:** At any week $n$, the events $U_n$ and $B_n$ form a partition of $\Omega$:
>
> $$U_n \cup B_n = \Omega, \qquad U_n \cap B_n = \emptyset, \qquad P(U_n) + P(B_n) = 1.$$
>
> **Transition probabilities:**
>
> $$P(U_{n+1} \mid U_n) = 0.8, \qquad P(B_{n+1} \mid U_n) = 0.2,$$
>
> $$P(U_{n+1} \mid B_n) = 0.4, \qquad P(B_{n+1} \mid B_n) = 0.6.$$
>
> **Initial condition (starting up-to-date):**
>
> $$P(U_0) = 1, \qquad P(B_0) = 0.$$
>
> Because $\{U_n, B_n\}$ partitions $\Omega$ for every $n$, the **Total Probability Theorem** can be applied at each step.

---

> **[Step 2] Key Observation / Diagram**
>
> The structure is a two-state system that evolves over time. At each week, we branch from the current state into the two possible next states. Below is the transition diagram.
>
> ```
>          0.8 (stay U)                0.4 (recover)
>       ┌──────────────────────┐    ┌──────────────────┐
>       ▼                      │    ▼                  │
>  ┌─────────┐  0.2(fall behind) ┌─────────┐  0.6(stay B) │
>  │  U (up) │ ─────────────────▶│  B (behind) │──────────┘
>  └─────────┘                  └─────────┘
>       ▲                            │
>       └────────────────────────────┘
>              0.4 (recover)
> ```
>
> At week $n+1$, Alice is up-to-date if she was up-to-date at week $n$ **and** stayed so, or she was behind **and** caught up. This is precisely the situation where the Total Probability Theorem applies.

---

> **[Step 3] Derivation — Part (1): Recurrence Relation**
>
> Since $\{U_n, B_n\}$ is a partition of $\Omega$ with $P(U_n), P(B_n) > 0$ (for $n \geq 1$), the Total Probability Theorem gives:
>
> $$P(U_{n+1}) = P(U_n)\,P(U_{n+1} \mid U_n) + P(B_n)\,P(U_{n+1} \mid B_n).$$
>
> Substituting the transition probabilities:
>
> $$P(U_{n+1}) = 0.8\,P(U_n) + 0.4\,P(B_n).$$
>
> Since $P(B_n) = 1 - P(U_n)$, this simplifies to a **first-order linear recurrence** in $P(U_n)$ alone:
>
> $$P(U_{n+1}) = 0.8\,P(U_n) + 0.4\,(1 - P(U_n)) = 0.4\,P(U_n) + 0.4.$$
>
> This is the key recurrence relation:
>
> $$\boxed{P(U_{n+1}) = 0.4\,P(U_n) + 0.4}, \qquad P(U_0) = 1.$$

---

> **[Step 4] Computation — Part (2): Step-by-step values (starting up-to-date)**
>
> Applying the recurrence iteratively:
>
> **Week 1:**
> $$P(U_1) = 0.4 \times P(U_0) + 0.4 = 0.4 \times 1 + 0.4 = 0.80.$$
>
> Cross-check using the direct formula:
> $$P(U_1) = P(U_0)\cdot 0.8 + P(B_0)\cdot 0.4 = 1\cdot 0.8 + 0\cdot 0.4 = 0.80. \checkmark$$
>
> **Week 2:**
> $$P(U_2) = 0.4 \times P(U_1) + 0.4 = 0.4 \times 0.80 + 0.4 = 0.320 + 0.400 = 0.720.$$
>
> **Week 3:**
> $$P(U_3) = 0.4 \times P(U_2) + 0.4 = 0.4 \times 0.720 + 0.4 = 0.288 + 0.400 = 0.688.$$
>
> *(This matches the result in Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis, Example 1.15.)*

---

> **[Step 5] Derivation — Part (3): Long-run behavior and steady state**
>
> The recurrence $P(U_{n+1}) = 0.4\,P(U_n) + 0.4$ is a first-order affine map with multiplier $|0.4| < 1$. By the **fixed-point theorem for contractions**, the sequence $P(U_n)$ converges to the unique fixed point $\pi_U$ satisfying
>
> $$\pi_U = 0.4\,\pi_U + 0.4.$$
>
> Solving for $\pi_U$:
>
> $$\pi_U - 0.4\,\pi_U = 0.4 \implies 0.6\,\pi_U = 0.4 \implies \pi_U = \frac{0.4}{0.6} = \frac{2}{3}.$$
>
> **Derivation from the Total Probability Theorem:**
>
> In steady state, $P(U_{n+1}) = P(U_n) = \pi_U$, so the Total Probability Theorem equation becomes:
>
> $$\pi_U = 0.8\,\pi_U + 0.4\,(1 - \pi_U).$$
>
> Expanding:
>
> $$\pi_U = 0.8\,\pi_U + 0.4 - 0.4\,\pi_U = 0.4\,\pi_U + 0.4.$$
>
> This yields the same fixed-point equation, giving $\pi_U = \dfrac{2}{3} \approx 0.667$.
>
> Similarly, $\pi_B = 1 - \pi_U = \dfrac{1}{3}$.
>
> **Closed-form solution:** The general solution to the recurrence $P(U_{n+1}) = 0.4\,P(U_n) + 0.4$ is:
>
> $$P(U_n) = \frac{2}{3} + \left(P(U_0) - \frac{2}{3}\right)(0.4)^n.$$
>
> Since $|0.4| < 1$, the term $(0.4)^n \to 0$ exponentially fast, confirming convergence to $\pi_U = \frac{2}{3}$ regardless of the initial state.

---

> **[Step 6] Computation — Part (4): Starting behind ($P(B_0) = 1$)**
>
> Now $P(U_0) = 0$ and $P(B_0) = 1$. The same recurrence applies:
>
> $$P(U_{n+1}) = 0.4\,P(U_n) + 0.4.$$
>
> **Week 1:**
> $$P(U_1) = 0.4 \times 0 + 0.4 = 0.400.$$
>
> **Week 2:**
> $$P(U_2) = 0.4 \times 0.400 + 0.4 = 0.160 + 0.400 = 0.560.$$
>
> **Week 3:**
> $$P(U_3) = 0.4 \times 0.560 + 0.4 = 0.224 + 0.400 = 0.624.$$
>
> **Comparison:**
>
> | $n$ | $P(U_n)$: start U | $P(U_n)$: start B |
> |-----|-------------------|-------------------|
> | 0   | 1.000             | 0.000             |
> | 1   | 0.800             | 0.400             |
> | 2   | 0.720             | 0.560             |
> | 3   | 0.688             | 0.624             |
> | $\infty$ | $2/3 \approx 0.667$ | $2/3 \approx 0.667$ |
>
> **Discussion:** The initial condition significantly affects $P(U_n)$ in the short run. Starting up-to-date gives $P(U_3) = 0.688$; starting behind gives only $P(U_3) = 0.624$. However, both sequences converge to the **same steady-state value** $\pi_U = \frac{2}{3}$, because the convergence factor $0.4^n \to 0$ erases the memory of the initial condition. This is a characteristic property of **ergodic** two-state Markov chains: the long-run behavior is independent of where you start.

---

> **[Step 7] Verification**
>
> At every step, the probabilities satisfy the axioms:
>
> $$P(U_n) + P(B_n) = 1 \quad \text{for all } n. \checkmark$$
>
> For example, at $n = 3$ (starting U): $0.688 + 0.312 = 1.00$. $\checkmark$
>
> The steady-state values: $\pi_U + \pi_B = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = 1$. $\checkmark$
>
> All intermediate values lie in $[0, 1]$. $\checkmark$
>
> The closed-form formula can also be verified:
> $$P(U_3) = \frac{2}{3} + \left(1 - \frac{2}{3}\right)(0.4)^3 = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{3}(0.064) = \frac{2}{3} + 0.02133 \approx 0.688. \checkmark$$

---

**Answers:**

**Part (1):** Recurrence relation:
$$P(U_{n+1}) = 0.4\,P(U_n) + 0.4, \qquad P(U_0) = 1.$$

**Part (2):** Starting up-to-date:
$$P(U_1) = 0.80, \quad P(U_2) = 0.72, \quad P(U_3) = 0.688.$$

**Part (3):** $P(U_n) \to \pi_U = \dfrac{2}{3} \approx 0.667$ as $n \to \infty$, independent of the initial condition.

**Part (4):** Starting behind: $P(U_3) = 0.624$. The initial condition affects short-run probabilities but not the long-run steady state.
