# Hard Problem

There are three processes, A, B, and C, in a production line. Each product goes through one of these processes with the following probabilities:
* Process A: 50%
* Process B: 30%
* Process C: 20%
The defect rates for each process are as follows:
* Process A: 1%
* Process B: 3%
* Process C: 6%
Given a randomly chosen product:
(1) Find the overall probability that the product is defective. (Total probability)
(2) Find the probability that the product came from Process C, given that it is defective. (Bayes' Theorem)
(3) Determine if the "process type" and "defect status" are independent. (Independence)
Additional Condition:
Suppose 4 products are inspected at the same time. Each product is independent.
(4) Find the probability that exactly 2 products are defective among the 4. (Counting)
(5) Find the probability that at least one product is defective among the 4. (Total probability)
(6) Given that there is at least 1 defective product among the 4, find the probability that the first product is defective. (Bayes' Theorem + Independence)
