# Topic: Bernoulli Trials, Independence, and Outage Probability

**Problem:**
An Internet Service Provider (ISP) has installed $c$ modems to serve $n$ customers. Each customer, independently of others, requires a connection at any given time with a probability p. 

Consider a specific scenario where n = 10 and p = 0.3. 

1. If the ISP installs only c = 2 modems, calculate the exact probability of an "outage," defined as the event where the number of customers needing a connection exceeds the number of available modems. 
2. Now, suppose the ISP introduces a "priority system" where customers are partitioned into two groups: 5 "Premium" users and 5 "Regular" users. If an outage occurs only when more than 2 modems are needed in total, but Premium users always get access first, find the conditional probability that a specific Regular user fails to connect given that exactly 3 customers (of any type) require a connection. 
3. Prove that the probability of a head in k successful Bernoulli trials satisfies the binomial formula: ∑(k=0 to n) nCk * p^k * (1-p)^(n-k) = 1.
