## Problem 3: Bayesian Inference with Detection Uncertainty

**[Problem Scenario]**
A software security team consists of 5 experts: **2 Seniors and 3 Juniors**. A project group of **3 members** is randomly selected. To verify the team composition, a scanning system is used, which detects the presence of at least one Senior (Event $A$). The system has the following reliability:
- $P(A | \text{Senior} \ge 1) = 0.9$ (True Positive)
- $P(A | \text{Senior} = 0) = 0.2$ (False Positive)

Let **Event $B$** be the case where **exactly two Juniors** (and thus exactly one Senior) are selected. Given that the system signaled **"Senior Detected" (Event $A$)**, calculate the posterior probability $P(B|A)$.
