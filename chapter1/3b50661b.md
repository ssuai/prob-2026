Problem 3 (Hard)

Topic: Discrete Random Variables (Poisson) and Bayes' Rule

A semiconductor manufacturing factory produces silicon wafers. The process can be in one of two operational states: "Normal" or "Abnormal".

- The process operates in the "Normal" state with a probability of 0.90
- The process operates in the "Abnormal" state with a probability of 0.10.

The number of microscopic defects found on a single wafer follows a Poisson distribution.

- When the process is in the "Normal" state, the average number of defects per wafer is λ=1.
- When the process is in the "Abnormal" state, the average number of defects per wafer is λ=5.

A quality control engineer inspects a randomly selected wafer and finds exactly 3 defects on it. Given this observation, what is the probability that the manufacturing process was in the "Abnormal" state when this wafer was produced?

(Note: You may leave your final answer in terms of the mathematical constant e.)
