# Exercise 2: Total Probability and Bayes' Rule

A chess player's probability of winning depends on the type of opponent they face. Opponents are classified into three distinct types:
- **Type 1**: Represents 50% of all players. The probability of winning against them is 0.3.
- **Type 2**: Represents 25% of all players. The probability of winning against them is 0.4.
- **Type 3**: Represents 25% of all players. The probability of winning against them is 0.5.

**Questions:**
1. If you play against a randomly selected opponent, what is the total probability that you will win the game?
2. Suppose you have just won a game. What is the probability that your opponent was a Type 2 player?
