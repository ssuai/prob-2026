[LEVEL 2: HARD]
Topic: Bayes' Rule and Sequential Conditional Probability
Context: Two-Stage Spam Email Filtering

An email service classifies incoming emails as spam or not spam.
From past data, the prior probability that an email is spam is:

  P(A)   = 0.4   (spam)
  P(A^c) = 0.6   (not spam)

The service uses two sequential checks:

  Check 1 (Event B): A keyword detector scans the email.
    P(B | A)   = 0.8   (spam emails are likely flagged)
    P(B | A^c) = 0.2   (normal emails are rarely flagged)

  Check 2 (Event C): A sender blacklist check is performed
  AFTER Check 1 has already flagged the email (event B).
    P(C | B ∩ A)   = 0.7   (spam senders are likely blacklisted)
    P(C | B ∩ A^c) = 0.1   (normal senders are rarely blacklisted)

An incoming email is first flagged by Check 1 (event B).
Then, the same email is also flagged by Check 2 (event C).

1. Using Bayes' rule, find the updated probability that the email
   is spam after Check 1 alone, P(A | B):
   (a) ________

2. Using P(A | B) as the new prior, and the given probabilities
   P(C | B ∩ A) and P(C | B ∩ A^c), apply Bayes' rule again to
   find the probability that the email is spam after BOTH checks,
   P(A | B ∩ C):
   (b) ________

3. Compare P(A), P(A | B), and P(A | B ∩ C). What do you observe
   about the probability of spam as more evidence is collected?
   (c) ________

---

Answer:
   (a) P(A | B) = 8/11
   (b) P(A | B ∩ C) = 56/59
   (c) P(A) = 2/5  <  P(A | B) = 8/11  <  P(A | B ∩ C) = 56/59
       Each new piece of evidence increases the probability of spam.
