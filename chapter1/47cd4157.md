### [Hard] 

A specialized software company utilizes two types of automated code-testing engines: **Engine A** and **Engine B**. 
* 60% of the company’s software modules are analyzed by **Engine A**, which has a **5%** probability of failing to detect a critical bug in a single scan.
* 40% of the modules are analyzed by **Engine B**, which has a **25%** probability of failing to detect a critical bug in a single scan.

A manager randomly selects a module that was processed by an unknown engine and discovers that the engine **failed** to detect a critical bug during its **first** scan. Given this specific observation, calculate the **conditional probability** that the same engine will also **fail** to detect a critical bug during a **second**, independent scan of the same module. 

(Note: Assume that conditioned on the type of engine being used, the results of successive scans are independent.)
