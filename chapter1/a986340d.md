# Intermediate Problem

A factory produces light bulbs from two machines. Machine A produces 60% of the bulbs, and Machine B produces 40%. The probability that a bulb is defective is 5% for Machine A and 10% for Machine B.

1. What is the probability that a randomly selected bulb is defective? (Use the law of total probability)
2. Given that a bulb is defective, what is the probability that it was produced by Machine B? (Use Bayes' rule)
3. Define appropriate events and describe the probabilistic model used in this problem.
