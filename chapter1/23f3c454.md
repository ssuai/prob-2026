# Intermediate Problem (Independence & Conditional Probability)

A fair die is rolled twice.

Let
A = "first roll is even"
B = "sum of two rolls is 8"

1. Find P(A) and P(B)
2. Find P(A ∩ B)
3. Determine whether A and B are independent

## Solution

Total outcomes = 36

A: first roll even → {2,4,6}
→ 3 × 6 = 18 outcomes
P(A) = 18/36 = 1/2

B: sum = 8 → (2,6),(3,5),(4,4),(5,3),(6,2)
→ 5 outcomes
P(B) = 5/36

A ∩ B:
(2,6),(4,4),(6,2)
→ 3 outcomes

P(A ∩ B) = 3/36 = 1/12

Check independence:
P(A)P(B) = (1/2)(5/36) = 5/72

Since 1/12 ≠ 5/72
→ Not independent
