# Intermediate Problem


In an inspection process, a product is inspected one by one. The probability of each product being defective is 0.1, and the products are independent of each other. The inspection continues until the first defective product is found.
(1) Find the probability that the first defective product is found on the 3rd product. (Independence + counting)
(2) Find the probability that at least one defective product is found among the first 3 products. (Total probability)
(3) Find the probability that exactly 1 defective product is found among the first 3 products. (Counting)
(4) Given that exactly 1 defective product is found among the first 3 products, find the probability that it is the 3rd product. (Bayes' Theorem)
