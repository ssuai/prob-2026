[LEVEL 3: ADVANCED CHALLENGE]
Topic: Conditional Independence and Bernoulli Trials
CS Context: Network Packet Transmission / Error Correction

A network bridge transmits a sequence of bits. Due to thermal noise, 
each bit has a probability 'p' of being flipped (0 to 1 or 1 to 0) 
independently of all other bits. 

To increase reliability, the bridge uses a Triple Modular Redundancy 
(TMR) scheme: it sends the same bit three times (e.g., to send a "1", 
it transmits "111"). The receiver uses a majority-vote decoding rule: 
if at least two of the three received bits are "1", it decodes the 
message as "1".

TASK:
Assume the bridge intends to send the bit "1" (encoded as "111").

1. Derive the probability P(F) that the receiver FAILS to decode the 
   correct bit (decodes it as "0") in terms of 'p'.

2. Given that the receiver SUCCESSFULLY decoded the bit as "1", what 
   is the conditional probability that NO BIT ERRORS actually occurred 
   during the transmission of the three bits? 
   Show your full derivation.
