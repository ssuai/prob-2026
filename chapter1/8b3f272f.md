# Problem 2: The Fitness Wearable False-Positive

**Problem Statement:**
Imagine you are tracking your weight training recovery using a new smart fitness strap. The strap uses sensors to monitor your Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and predicts whether your Central Nervous System (CNS) is severely overtrained.

Based on the manufacturer's data, the sensor is accurate 95% of the time. Specifically:
- If you are actually overtrained, the sensor will correctly trigger an "Overtraining Warning" with a probability of 0.95.
- If you are fully recovered, the sensor will incorrectly trigger a false alarm with a probability of 0.05.

From your personal training logs, you know that you strictly manage your volume, so you are only genuinely overtrained 5% of the time on any given day.

**Question:**
One morning, the strap triggers an "Overtraining Warning." Given this observed effect, what is the actual probability that your CNS is genuinely overtrained?
