[LEVEL 1: EASY]
Topic: Conditional Probability and the Multiplication Rule
Context: Sequential Drawing Without Replacement

A box contains 4 red balls and 6 blue balls. Two balls are drawn
one at a time without replacement. Let the events be defined as:

  A_1 = {1st ball drawn is red}
  A_2 = {2nd ball drawn is red}

1. The probability that the 1st ball drawn is red, P(A_1), is:
   (a) ________

2. Given that the 1st ball drawn was red, the conditional probability
   that the 2nd ball is also red, P(A_2 | A_1), is:
   (b) ________

3. Using the multiplication rule, the probability that both balls
   drawn are red, P(A_1 ∩ A_2), is:
   (c) ________

---

Answer:
   (a) 4/10 = 2/5
   (b) 3/9 = 1/3
   (c) 2/15
