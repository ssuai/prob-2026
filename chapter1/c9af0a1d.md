Problem 3 (Hard)

A clinical trial committee must form a review panel of 5 members chosen from a pool of 12 candidates: 5 physicians, 4 statisticians, and 3 ethicists. The panel must include at least 2 physicians and exactly 1 ethicist.

	(a) How many valid panels satisfy these constraints?

	(b) If the 5-person panel is chosen completely at random (with no constraints), what is the probability that the resulting panel happens to satisfy both constraints?

	(c) Given that the randomly chosen panel contains exactly 1 ethicist, what is the conditional probability it also contains at least 2 physicians?
