# Problem (Hard — Counting, Independence & Conditional Probability)

A box contains 5 distinct cards labeled \(1, 2, 3, 4, 5\). Two cards are drawn without replacement and placed in order.

Define the following events:
- \(A\): The sum of the two drawn numbers is even  
- \(B\): The first card drawn is greater than the second  

1. Compute \(P(A)\), \(P(B)\), and \(P(A \cap B)\) using a counting-based sample space.
2. Are events \(A\) and \(B\) independent? Prove your conclusion.
3. Given that the sum is even, compute \(P(B \mid A)\).
4. Suppose you are told that the first card is greater than the second. Compute \(P(A \mid B)\) and interpret the result.

## Assumptions / Given
- All ordered pairs of distinct cards are equally likely.
- Explicit counting of outcomes is required (do not shortcut using symmetry without justification).
