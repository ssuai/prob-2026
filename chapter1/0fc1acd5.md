A factory uses two machines, M1 and M2, to manufacture products. Machine M1 produces 

60% (3/5) of the total items, and M2 produces 40% (2/5).

The probability that an item produced by M1 is defective (Event D) is 5% (1/20), and the 

probability that an item produced by M2 is defective is 10% (1/10).



1. If an item is selected at random from the total production, find the probability 
P(D) that it is defective.
2. Given that the randomly selected item is found to be defective, find the posterior probability 

P(M1∣D) that it was produced by machine M1.
