Level: Intermediate (Bayes’ Rule & Total Probability)
The Scenario:
A medical lab has developed a new test for a rare "Study Fatigue" virus that affects 2% of the student population.

If a student has the virus, the test returns a positive result 95% of the time (True Positive).

If a student does not have the virus, the test returns a positive result 10% of the time (False Positive).

The Question:

A student is picked at random and tested. What is the total probability that the test result is positive?

If a student’s test result comes back positive, what is the probability that they actually have the virus?
