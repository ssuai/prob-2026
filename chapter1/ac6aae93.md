## Problem 2 (Intermediate)
In a chemistry experiment, two types of reagent are used to verify the result sample:

- Reagent $\alpha$ is used for 70% of the samples: $P(\alpha) = 0.7$
- Reagent $\beta$ is used for 30% of the samples: $P(\beta) = 0.3$

- Probability that Reagent $\alpha$ correctly identifies the result: $P(C \mid \alpha) = 0.95$
- Probability that Reagent $\beta$ correctly identifies the result: $P(C \mid \beta) = 0.80$

Calculate **the total probability $P(C)$ that a randomly chosen sample** is correctly verified. Then, given that a sample has been correctly identified, determine **the posterior probability that Reagent $\beta$** was the one used in the experiment.
