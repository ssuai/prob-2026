# Problem 2 (Intermediate)

A factory has three machines \(M_1, M_2,\) and \(M_3\) that produce the same type of item.

- \(40\%\) of the items are produced by \(M_1\)
- \(35\%\) of the items are produced by \(M_2\)
- \(25\%\) of the items are produced by \(M_3\)

The probability that an item is defective depends on which machine produced it:

- \(P(D \mid M_1) = 0.02\)
- \(P(D \mid M_2) = 0.05\)
- \(P(D \mid M_3) = 0.04\)

where \(D\) is the event that a randomly selected item is defective.

Answer the following questions.

## (a)

Explain why the events \(M_1, M_2, M_3\) form a partition of the sample space.

## (b)

Use the law of total probability to find \(P(D)\), the probability that a randomly selected item is defective.

## (c)

Briefly explain why \(P(D)\) cannot be found by simply adding the three conditional probabilities

\[
P(D \mid M_1), \; P(D \mid M_2), \; P(D \mid M_3).
\]
