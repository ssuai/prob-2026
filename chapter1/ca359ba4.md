A cryptographic channel transmits a sequence of independent binary bits. The transmission uses one of three hidden keys, partitioned as K1, K2, and K3. 
Prior probabilities dictate that key K1 is chosen with P(K1)=1/6 ,K2 with P(K2)=1/3, and K3 with P(K3)=1/2. 
Each key defines the probability of successfully transmitting a "1" bit independently for each position in the sequence: P(1∣K1)=0.2P, P(1∣K2)=0.5, and P(1∣K3)=0.8P. 
You intercept a 4-bit sequence and observe exactly three "1"s and one "0" (in any permutation). Calculate the exact posterior probability that key K3 was used to transmit this sequence.
