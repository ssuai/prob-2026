# Practice Problem 3: Inference Using Bayes' Rule

### [Problem]
You are designing an email spam filtering system. Statistically, $20\%$ of all emails are spam ($A$).
* Given an email is spam ($A$), the filter correctly identifies it as 'spam' ($B$) with a probability of $0.95$.
* Given an email is not spam ($A^c$), the filter incorrectly flags it as 'spam' ($B$) with a probability of $0.10$ (False Alarm).

If an email is **flagged as 'spam' by the filter**, what is the **posterior probability $P(A|B)$** that the email is actually spam? (Round your answer to two decimal places.)
