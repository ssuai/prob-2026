### Hard (Level: Advanced) - Bayes' Rule in Manufacturing

Problem:

A high-tech factory uses three different machines ($M_1, M_2, M_3$) to produce the same microchip. The production distribution and defect rates for each machine are as follows:
- Machine 1 ($M_1$): Produces 50% of the total output, with a defect rate of 2%.
- Machine 2 ($M_2$): Produces 30% of the total output, with a defect rate of 5%.
- Machine 3 ($M_3$): Produces 20% of the total output, with a defect rate of 10%.

A quality control manager randomly selects one machine and then picks two chips produced by that specific machine. If both chips are found to be defective, what is the probability that they were produced by Machine 3 ($M_3$)?
(Assume the defects in the chips from the same machine are independent given the machine.)
