### The Scenario
Consider a fair "Wheel of Fortune" that is continuously calibrated from $0$ to $1$. When you spin the wheel, the outcome can be any real number within the interval $\Omega = [0, 1]$. Assume that the probability of the wheel stopping in any specific sub-interval is proportional to the length of that interval.

### The Problem - Easy
1. What is the probability $P(A)$ that the wheel stops exactly on the number $0.5$? Justify your answer based on the Additivity Axiom.
2. What is the probability that the wheel stops at a value between $0.3$ and $0.7$ (i.e., the event $[0.3, 0.7]$)? 
3. What is the probability that the wheel stops in the range $[0.1, 0.2]$ or the range $[0.8, 0.95]$?
