## Problem 3 (Hard)

A city operates an autonomous shuttle service where the arrivals of buses at a specific downtown stop follow a Poisson process with an average rate of $\lambda = 4$ buses per hour. An inspector arrives at the bus stop at a completely arbitrary time $t^*$ that is strictly independent of the bus arrival process. Let $T_p$ denote the time elapsed from the departure of the last bus until the inspector's arrival, and let $T_f$ denote the time the inspector must wait from $t^*$ until the arrival of the next bus. Define the total interarrival interval strictly containing the inspector's arrival as $W = T_p + T_f$. Rigorously derive the expected value of this total interval, $E[W]$, and mathematically prove why this expected interval length inherently differs from the standard average time between consecutive bus arrivals, explicitly identifying the underlying probabilistic paradox that governs this behavior.
