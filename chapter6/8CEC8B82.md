Hard: Multi-Core Interrupt HandlingA dual-core processor handles hardware interrupts. Core 1 generates interrupts according to a Poisson process with a rate of $\lambda_1 = 4$ interrupts per millisecond. Core 2 generates interrupts according to an independent Poisson process with a rate of $\lambda_2 = 2$ interrupts per millisecond. The system merges these into a single unified queue.Question: Formulate the Probability Density Function (PDF) for the time $T$ (in milliseconds) until exactly the 5th interrupt arrives in the unified queue. What is the expected time to reach this 5th interrupt?Key to Solving:This requires combining the merging property of Poisson processes with continuous time distributions.Merge the two independent Poisson processes by adding their rates to find the unified system rate ($\lambda = \lambda_1 + \lambda_2$).Recognize that the time until the $k$-th arrival in a Poisson process follows an Erlang PDF.Substitute your unified $\lambda$ and $k = 5$ into the Erlang PDF formula:$$f_T(t) = \frac{\lambda^k t^{k-1} e^{-\lambda t}}{(k-1)!}$$Use the properties of the Erlang distribution (or sum of independent Exponential random variables) to determine the expected time.
