# Problem 3 — Hard

**Difficulty:** Hard
**Concepts tested:** Splitting of a Poisson process, merging of Poisson processes, competing (minimum of) independent exponentials, Erlang distribution, Central Limit Theorem approximation

---

## Scenario

A 24‑hour call center receives **technical‑support** calls and **billing** calls.

- Technical‑support calls arrive as a **Poisson process** with rate
  $\lambda_T = 4$ calls per hour.
- Billing calls arrive as an **independent Poisson process** with rate
  $\lambda_B = 6$ calls per hour.

When a technical call arrives it is, **independently**, classified as *"urgent"*
with probability $q = 0.25$ and *"routine"* with probability $1-q = 0.75$.

---

## Questions

**(a) (Splitting.)** Describe the process of **urgent technical calls** and the
process of **routine technical calls**. Give the rate of each, state that they
are independent Poisson processes, and compute the probability that **exactly 1
urgent technical call** arrives in a given **2‑hour** period.

**(b) (Merging.)** Consider the **combined** stream of *all* calls (technical +
billing). What kind of process is it and what is its rate? Compute the
probability that the combined stream produces **no calls at all** during a
**15‑minute** period.

**(c) (Competing exponentials.)** At time $t_0$ the center is idle (no calls in
progress). Let $T_T$ be the waiting time until the next technical call and $T_B$
the waiting time until the next billing call. These are independent exponentials.
Find the distribution of $Z = \min\{T_T, T_B\}$ (the time until the *next call of
any type*), and find the probability that **the next call is a billing call**
(i.e. $P(T_B < T_T)$).

**(d) (Erlang + CLT.)** Let $W$ be the time (in hours) until the **40th billing
call** arrives, starting from an idle instant. Identify the distribution of $W$
and give $E[W]$ and $\operatorname{var}(W)$. Then use the **Central Limit
Theorem** to approximate $P(W \le 7)$. (You may express the answer using the
standard normal CDF $\Phi(\cdot)$ and then give a numerical value.)
