Hard Problem — Combined Bernoulli and Poisson Process
Customers arrive at a coffee shop according to a Poisson process with an average rate of 12 customers per hour.
Each customer independently buys a dessert with probability 0.35.
Assume the shop operates for 2 hours.
What is the probability that exactly 5 customers buy desserts during the 2-hour period?
What is the expected number of customers who buy desserts in 2 hours?
What is the probability that at least one customer buys a dessert during the first 30 minutes?
