📝 Problem 3 (Hard, Bernoulli + Poisson Combined Application) — Gym Member Scenario
A 24-hour gym is in operation. Member arrivals follow a Poisson process with λ=6\lambda = 6
λ=6/hour.
Each member independently engages in one of the following:

Weight training with probability 0.5
Cardio (treadmill) with probability 0.3
Yoga with probability 0.2

Each activity duration is i.i.d. exponential, with an average of 60 minutes (assumed equal for all).
(1) What is the distribution of the "weight training member arrival" process?
(2) What is the distribution of the "cardio member arrival" process?
(3) What is the probability that exactly 2 yoga members arrive during a 30-minute window? (Formula → numerical value)
(4) What is the probability that the next arriving member is a weight training member?
(5) There are currently 4 members in the gym (all exercising). Each leaves according to a memoryless exponential distribution. What is the probability that all 4 members leave within the next 30 minutes?
Hint: Find the probability that one member leaves within 30 minutes, then multiply (independence).
(6) Different scenario: With the same 4 members in the gym, what is the average time until the first member leaves?
Hint: Minimum of 4 i.i.d. exponentials.
