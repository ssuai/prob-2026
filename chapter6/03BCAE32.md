## Problem: Bernoulli Process Basics

A customer arrives at a small store during each minute with probability

\[
p=0.2.
\]

Assume arrivals in different minutes are independent. Let \(X_i\) be the indicator random variable for whether a customer arrives during minute \(i\):

\[
X_i=
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if a customer arrives during minute }i,\\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
\]

Let

\[
S_{10}=X_1+X_2+\cdots+X_{10}
\]

be the number of arrivals during the first \(10\) minutes. Also, let \(T\) be the time, in minutes, until the first arrival.

### Questions

1. What distribution does \(S_{10}\) follow?
2. Compute \(P(S_{10}=3)\).
3. Find \(E[S_{10}]\) and \(\operatorname{var}(S_{10})\).
4. What distribution does \(T\) follow?
5. Compute \(P(T=4)\).
6. Compute \(P(T>5)\).
7. Find \(E[T]\) and \(\operatorname{var}(T)\).

---

### Notes

If \(S\sim \operatorname{Binomial}(n,p)\), then

\[
P(S=k)=\binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k},
\]

\[
E[S]=np,
\qquad
\operatorname{var}(S)=np(1-p).
\]

If \(T\sim \operatorname{Geometric}(p)\), then

\[
P(T=t)=(1-p)^{t-1}p,\qquad t=1,2,\ldots
\]

\[
E[T]=\frac{1}{p},
\qquad
\operatorname{var}(T)=\frac{1-p}{p^2}.
\]
