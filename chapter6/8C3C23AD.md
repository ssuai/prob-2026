# Problem 1 (Easy Level): Boutique Customer Arrival Process

A small boutique models its customer arrivals using a discrete-time Bernoulli process with 1-minute time slots. The probability that a customer arrives (success) during any given 1-minute slot is $p = 0.2$.

1. Find the probability that exactly 3 customers arrive during a specific 10-minute interval.
2. Find the expected number of minutes up to and including the minute the first customer walks through the door (the expected waiting time for the first arrival).
