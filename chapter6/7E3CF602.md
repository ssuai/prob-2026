# Problem 3 (Hard)

At an airport baggage carousel, bags from two airlines (A and B) arrive as independent Poisson processes. Airline A arrives at rate 2 bags/min, Airline B at rate 3 bags/min.

(a) What distribution does the merged baggage arrival process follow? Find its rate.

(b) Given that one bag has just arrived from the merged process, find the probability that it belongs to Airline A.
(Hint: View the merged process as Poisson with rate λ_A + λ_B, and think of each arrival being independently Airline A with probability λ_A / (λ_A + λ_B).)

(c) Analyze which airline's bag arrives first using the competing exponentials framework. Find the probability that an Airline B bag arrives before an Airline A bag.

(d) A staff member left and returned to find no bag had arrived yet. They have already waited 2 minutes in total. Find the probability that they will need to wait at least 1 more minute for the first bag to arrive.

(e) Use the CLT to approximate the probability that the time until the first 60 bags arrive from the merged process exceeds 13 minutes.
