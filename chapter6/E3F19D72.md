Question:
A central database receives queries from two separate servers. Server A sends queries according to a Poisson process with a rate of $\lambda_A = 2$ queries per minute. Server B sends queries according to an independent Poisson process with a rate of $\lambda_B = 3$ queries per minute. These two streams are merged into a single queue.What is the expected waiting time (in minutes) until exactly the 10th query arrives in the merged queue, and what is the exact probability density function (PDF), $f_Y(y)$, for this continuous waiting time $y$?
