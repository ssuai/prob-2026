# Problem 2 — Intermediate

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Concepts tested:** Poisson process, Poisson PMF, exponential interarrival time, memorylessness, Erlang (k‑th arrival) distribution

---

## Scenario

At a highway toll booth, cars arrive according to a **Poisson process** with rate
$\lambda = 3$ cars per minute.

---

## Questions

**(a)** What is the probability that **exactly 2 cars** arrive during a given
**1‑minute** interval? What is the probability that **no car** arrives during a
**30‑second** interval?

**(b)** Let $T$ be the time (in minutes) until the **first** car arrives, measured
from an arbitrary instant. Write the PDF of $T$, and compute $P(T > 1)$.
Then, using **memorylessness**, find the probability that the booth waits more
than **1 additional minute** for the next car *given that* no car has arrived in
the previous $0.5$ minute.

**(c)** Let $Y_5$ be the time until the **5th** car arrives (an Erlang random
variable of order 5). State $E[Y_5]$ and $\operatorname{var}(Y_5)$, and write
down the PDF $f_{Y_5}(y)$ explicitly with the numerical value of $\lambda$
substituted in.
