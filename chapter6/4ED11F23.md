# Problem 2 — Intermediate | Poisson Process

## Problem

Customers arrive at a store according to a Poisson process
with rate lambda = 3 per hour.

**(1)** Find the probability that exactly 2 customers arrive in a 1-hour interval.

**(2)** Find the probability that no customers arrive in a 30-minute interval.

**(3)** Let T = time until the first customer arrives (in hours).
Find E[T] and P(T > 0.5).

**(4)** Let Y_4 = time until the 4th customer arrives.
Find E[Y_4] and var(Y_4).

---

## Solution

### Part (1)

N_1 ~ Poisson(lambda * tau = 3 * 1 = 3)

P(N_1 = 2) = e^(-3) * 3^2 / 2!
           = e^(-3) * 9 / 2
           = **0.2240**

### Part (2)

tau = 0.5 hr,  lambda*tau = 3 * 0.5 = 1.5

P(N_{0.5} = 0) = e^(-1.5) * (1.5)^0 / 0!
              = e^(-1.5)
              = **0.2231**

### Part (3)

T ~ Exponential(lambda = 3)

E[T] = 1/lambda = 1/3 hr = **20 min**

P(T > 0.5) = e^(-lambda * 0.5) = e^(-3 * 0.5) = e^(-1.5) = **0.2231**

### Part (4)

Y_4 = T_1 + T_2 + T_3 + T_4  (sum of 4 i.i.d. Exp(3))
   -> Erlang of order k=4, rate lambda=3

E[Y_4]   = k / lambda = 4/3 hr = **80 min**

var(Y_4) = k / lambda^2 = 4/9 hr^2
