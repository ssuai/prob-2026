**Problem Statement:**
A deep-space observatory detects cosmic rays according to a continuous Poisson process with a constant rate $\lambda$. A specific experiment requires capturing exactly $5$ cosmic rays to achieve statistical significance. Let $T_{total}$ be the exact time the experiment concludes (the arrival time of the 5th cosmic ray). 
1. Suppose the observatory begins operating at time $t = 0$. By time $t = 10$, exactly $2$ cosmic rays have been recorded. Calculate the conditional expected value of $T_{total}$ given this information.
2. Suppose instead that you are informed that the 2nd cosmic ray arrived *exactly* at $t = 10$. Does the expected value of $T_{total}$ differ from your answer in part 1? Explain the structural reasoning behind your answer using the properties of the Poisson process.
