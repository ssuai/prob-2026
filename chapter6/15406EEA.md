**Problem Statement:**
Consider a communication channel that transmits discrete packets. The transmission operates as a Bernoulli process, where each packet transmission is an independent trial that succeeds with probability $p$ (where $0 < p < 1$) and fails with probability $1 - p$. Let $T$ be the number of trials required to achieve the first successful transmission.
1. Derive the exact probability that the first success occurs on an even-numbered trial. 
2. Evaluate the limit of this probability as $p$ approaches $1$ and as $p$ approaches $0$. Provide a physical interpretation for both limiting cases.
