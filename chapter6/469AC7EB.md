### Problem 03 — Difficulty: Hard
**Topic**: Poisson Merging, Splitting, Competing Exponentials, $E[\max]$, CLT  


#### Background

A city traffic control center monitors speed violations from two independent roads.

- **Road A**: Vehicles pass according to a Poisson process at rate $\lambda_A = 4$ vehicles/hr. Each vehicle independently commits a speed violation with probability $p = 0.25$.
- **Road B**: Vehicles pass according to a Poisson process at rate $\lambda_B = 8$ vehicles/hr. Each vehicle independently commits a speed violation with probability $q = 0.25$.

All violating vehicles are monitored by a unified surveillance system. Each detected violation is independently classified as **Type I** (immediate enforcement) with probability $r = 0.2$, or **Type II** (deferred enforcement) with probability $0.8$.

---

**(a)** Two officers (Officer X and Officer Y) begin standby at 9:00 AM. Officer X dispatches when the first Type I violation is detected; Officer Y dispatches when the first Type II violation is detected. Let $T_X$ and $T_Y$ denote their respective waiting times.

1. Find $P(T_X < T_Y)$: the probability that Officer X dispatches before Officer Y.
2. Find $E[M]$ where $M = \max\{T_X, T_Y\}$ is the time at which both officers have dispatched.

**(b)** The control center operates for 3 hours before shift change. Using the CLT, approximate the probability that the number of Type I enforcements during the 3-hour period is at most 4.

> *Standard normal table: $\Phi(1.645) = 0.95$, $\Phi(1) = 0.8413$, $\Phi(0.5) = 0.6915$, $\Phi(2) = 0.9772$*

**(c)** Full data collection begins only after $M = \max\{T_X, T_Y\}$, i.e., after both officers have dispatched at least once. Given that data collection has started, find the probability that no Type I enforcement occurs during the next 2 hours.

> *Hint: Think about what property applies to the Poisson process after the random time $M$.*
