# Problem 3 — Hard

## Topic: Splitting of a Poisson Process and Erlang Waiting Time

Customers arrive at a store according to a Poisson process with rate

\[
\lambda=12 \text{ customers per hour}.
\]

Each customer independently buys a product with probability

\[
p=0.25.
\]

Customers who buy a product form one split Poisson process, and customers who do not buy a product form another split Poisson process.

Answer the following questions.

1. Find the arrival rate of customers who buy a product.
2. Find the arrival rate of customers who do not buy a product.
3. Find the probability that exactly 5 customers buy a product during one hour.
4. Let \(Y_4\) be the waiting time until the 4th product-buying customer arrives. What distribution does \(Y_4\) follow?
5. Find \(E[Y_4]\) and \(Var(Y_4)\).
6. Approximate the probability that the 4th product-buying customer arrives within 90 minutes.
