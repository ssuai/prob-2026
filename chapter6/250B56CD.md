# Problem 1 — Easy

**Difficulty:** Easy
**Concepts tested:** Bernoulli process, Binomial random variable, Geometric random variable

---

## Scenario

A small bakery's online ordering page receives a visitor in each **1‑minute slot**.
In every slot, **independently**, a visitor places an order with probability
$p = 0.2$ (a "success"); otherwise no order is placed. This is a Bernoulli
process with parameter $p = 0.2$.

---

## Questions

**(a)** Over a fixed window of $n = 10$ minutes, let $S$ be the number of orders
placed. What is the probability that **exactly 3 orders** are placed?
Also state $E[S]$ and $\operatorname{var}(S)$.

**(b)** Let $T$ be the number of minutes (slots), counted from the start and
including the slot of the first order, **up to and including the first order**.
What is the probability that the first order occurs **exactly in the 4th
minute**, i.e. $P(T = 4)$? What is the expected waiting time $E[T]$?

**(c)** Using the memorylessness of the Bernoulli process: given that **no order**
has been placed during the first 5 minutes, what is the probability that the
first order occurs in **one of the next 3 minutes** (minutes 6, 7, or 8)?
