**Topic:** Bernoulli Process — Pascal PMF and Fresh-Start Property
**Style:** [Scenario-based word problem]
**Source PDF:** 61.pdf (Section 6.1)

---

A basketball player makes each free throw independently with probability $p = 1/2$. Her free-throw attempts form a Bernoulli process. Let $Y_k$ = the attempt number at which she makes her $k$-th free throw (the $k$-th arrival time).

**1.** Using the Pascal PMF of order $k$:

$$p_{Y_k}(t) = \binom{t-1}{k-1} p^k (1-p)^{t-k}, \quad t = k, k+1, \ldots$$

- **(a)** Find the probability that she makes her **3rd** free throw on exactly the **5th** attempt, i.e., find $P(Y_3 = 5)$. $\quad$ **________**

**2.** She has just missed her first 2 free throw attempts (0 successes in 2 tries).

> *After observing $X_1, \ldots, X_n$, the future $X_{n+1}, X_{n+2}, \ldots$ is again a Bernoulli process with the same parameter $p$, independent of past trials.*

- **(b)** How many **additional** attempts should she expect to need in order to make exactly **3** free throws? $\quad$ **________**

---

### Answer:

**(a)** $P(Y_3 = 5) = \dfrac{3}{16}$

**(b)** Expected additional attempts $= 6$

### Explanation:

**Step 1.** Apply the Pascal PMF with $k = 3$, $t = 5$, $p = 1/2$:

$$P(Y_3 = 5) = \binom{4}{2}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^3\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2} = 6 \cdot \frac{1}{8} \cdot \frac{1}{4} = \frac{6}{32} = \frac{3}{16}$$

*Interpretation:* Among the first 4 attempts, exactly 2 are successes (she is 2-for-4), and the 5th attempt is the 3rd success.

**Step 2.** Invoke the Fresh-Start Property. After the 2 failed attempts, the future sequence $X_3, X_4, \ldots$ is a new Bernoulli process with parameter $p = 1/2$, completely independent of what happened before.

**Step 3.** Starting fresh, she still needs to accumulate **3 successes**. By the Pascal mean formula:

$$E[Y_3] = \frac{k}{p} = \frac{3}{1/2} = 6 \text{ additional attempts}$$

**Step 4.** Note that the 2 prior failures are irrelevant — the Fresh-Start Property ensures the process has no memory of past outcomes. The conditioning on "0 successes in first 2 trials" does not change the expected waiting time from the current point onward.
