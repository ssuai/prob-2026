## Problem: Poisson Process, Exponential Waiting Times, Splitting, and Merging

Calls arrive at a service center according to a Poisson process with rate

\[
\lambda=4
\]

calls per hour.

Let \(N(t)\) be the number of calls arriving during an interval of length \(t\) hours.

Let \(T_1\) be the time until the first call, and let \(Y_5\) be the time until the fifth call.

Each call is independently classified as urgent with probability

\[
0.3.
\]

A second independent call stream also arrives according to a Poisson process with rate

\[
\lambda_2=2
\]

calls per hour.

### Questions

1. What is the distribution of \(N(2)\)?
2. Compute \(P(N(2)=6)\).
3. Compute the probability that no calls arrive in the first \(30\) minutes.
4. Find the CDF and PDF of \(T_1\).
5. Find \(E[T_1]\) and \(\operatorname{var}(T_1)\).
6. Using the memorylessness property, compute the probability that, given no calls arrived in the first \(30\) minutes, the next call arrives more than \(15\) minutes later.

---

### Notes

For a Poisson process with rate \(\lambda\),

\[
P(N(t)=k)=e^{-\lambda t}\frac{(\lambda t)^k}{k!},
\qquad k=0,1,2,\ldots
\]

and

\[
E[N(t)]=\lambda t,
\qquad
\operatorname{var}(N(t))=\lambda t.
\]

The first arrival time follows an exponential distribution:

\[
P(T_1>t)=e^{-\lambda t},
\]

\[
F_{T_1}(t)=1-e^{-\lambda t},
\qquad t\ge 0,
\]

\[
f_{T_1}(t)=\lambda e^{-\lambda t},
\qquad t\ge 0.
\]

The \(k\)-th arrival time follows an Erlang distribution:

\[
f_{Y_k}(y)=\frac{\lambda^k y^{k-1}e^{-\lambda y}}{(k-1)!},
\qquad y\ge 0.
\]

For splitting a Poisson process:

\[
\lambda_{\text{urgent}}=\lambda p,
\qquad
\lambda_{\text{non-urgent}}=\lambda(1-p).
\]

For merging two independent Poisson processes:

\[
\lambda_{\text{merged}}=\lambda_1+\lambda_2.
\]
