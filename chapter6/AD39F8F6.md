## Problem

You check your email every hour throughout the workday.
From past experience, you know that each hour independently has a probability $p = 0.3$ of containing at least one new message worth reading.

**(a)** What is the probability that you receive a worth-reading message in **exactly 2 out of 5 hours**?

**(b)** On average, how many hours do you expect to wait until you receive your **first** worth-reading message? What is the variance of this waiting time?

**(c)** Suppose you checked at hours 1 through 3 and found nothing. What is the probability that you still have to wait **at least 2 more hours** for the first message?
