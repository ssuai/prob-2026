Easy: Digital Transmission ErrorsA communication link transmits 2000 independent bits of data per packet. Due to channel noise, each bit has a small probability of $p = 0.002$ of being corrupted during transmission.Question: Using the Poisson approximation to the Binomial distribution, calculate the probability that exactly 3 bits are corrupted in a single packet.Key to Solving: Identify this as a Bernoulli process where the number of trials ($n$) is large and the probability of success ($p$) is very small. Calculate the Poisson parameter $\lambda = np$, and then apply the standard Poisson PMF formula for $k = 3$
