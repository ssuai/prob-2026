# Question 3 (Hard): Erlang Distribution, Poisson Relationship, and Conditional Expectation

A cybersecurity monitoring system analyzes network anomalies. Anomalies arrive according to a Poisson process with a rate of $\lambda = 2$ anomalies per hour. The system is designed to trigger a major lockdown the moment the exactly $k = 4$th anomaly is detected. Let $Y_4$ be the total time (in hours) from startup ($t=0$) until the 4th anomaly arrives.

1. **[Probability via Poisson Relationship]** Calculate the exact probability that the lockdown is triggered *after* exactly 3 hours have passed. 
   *(Hint: Instead of integrating the Erlang PDF, use the relationship between the $k$-th arrival time $Y_k$ and the number of arrivals $N_t$ in time $t$. Express your answer using $e$.)*
2. **[Conditional Expectation]** Suppose exactly 2 hours have passed since startup, and the system has detected exactly 1 anomaly so far. What is the expected *total* time (from $t=0$) until the lockdown is triggered?
