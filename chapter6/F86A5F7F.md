## Problem

A small coffee shop near a university campus receives customer orders through two independent channels: walk-in customers and online orders.

- Walk-in customers arrive as a Poisson process with rate $\lambda_w = 4$ per hour.
- Online orders arrive as a Poisson process with rate $\lambda_o = 2$ per hour.

The shop opens at 9:00 AM.

**(a)** The barista wants to prepare a fresh batch of milk before the first order — from **either** channel — arrives. What is the distribution of the time until the first order arrives, and what is its expected value?

**(b)** The shop's espresso machine breaks down at 9:20 AM (i.e., at $t = 1/3$ hour). From that point, the shop can only serve **online orders** until the machine is fixed. What is the probability that **no online orders** arrive during the 15-minute repair window?

**(c)** Between 9:00 and 10:00 AM, what is the expected total number of orders, and what is the probability that **more than 8 orders** arrive in that hour?

> *Hint for (c): You may leave the answer as a sum, or use the Poisson PMF directly.*
