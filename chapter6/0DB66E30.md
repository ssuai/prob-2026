# Problem 3 — Hard | Poisson Merging, Competing Exponentials & CLT

## Problem

Two independent Poisson processes model arrivals at a server:
- Process A: rate lambda_a = 2 per minute
- Process B: rate lambda_b = 3 per minute

**(1)** The two processes are merged.
What is the distribution of the merged process?
Find the probability that at least 1 arrival occurs in a 0.5-minute interval.

**(2)** Each arrival is independently routed back to Process A (prob. 0.4)
or to Process B (prob. 0.6) — i.e., the merged process is split.
What are the rates of the two resulting split processes?

**(3)** Let T_a = time until the next arrival from Process A,
and T_b = time until the next arrival from Process B (independent exponentials).
Let Z = min{T_a, T_b}.
Find the PDF of Z and E[Z].

**(4)** The server processes jobs one by one. Each job takes an
exponential amount of time with rate mu = 5 per minute.
You are the 40th person in the queue.
Use the CLT to approximate P(your wait > 9 minutes).
(Use Phi(1.0) = 0.8413)

---

## Solution

### Part (1) — Merging

Merged process is Poisson with rate:
  lambda = lambda_a + lambda_b = 2 + 3 = **5 per minute**

P(at least 1 arrival in 0.5 min):
  = 1 - P(0 arrivals in 0.5 min)
  = 1 - e^(-5 * 0.5)
  = 1 - e^(-2.5)
  = **1 - 0.0821 = 0.9179**

### Part (2) — Splitting

The merged Poisson process (rate 5) is split with p = 0.4, 1-p = 0.6.
By the splitting property of Poisson processes:

  Rate of split A = 5 * 0.4 = **2 per minute**
  Rate of split B = 5 * 0.6 = **3 per minute**

(Recovers the original rates, as expected.)

### Part (3) — Competing Exponentials

T_a ~ Exp(lambda_a = 2),  T_b ~ Exp(lambda_b = 3)

Z = min{T_a, T_b}

By the competing exponentials result:
  Z ~ Exp(lambda_a + lambda_b) = Exp(5)

PDF:   f_Z(z) = 5 * e^(-5z),   z >= 0

E[Z] = 1 / (lambda_a + lambda_b) = 1/5 = **0.2 minutes**

### Part (4) — CLT Approximation

Each service time X_i ~ Exp(mu = 5)

  E[X_i]   = 1/mu = 1/5 = 0.2 min
  Var(X_i) = 1/mu^2 = 1/25

Total wait Y = X_1 + ... + X_40  (Erlang, order 40)

  E[Y]   = 40 * 0.2 = 8 min
  Var(Y) = 40 * (1/25) = 1.6 min^2
  SD(Y)  = sqrt(1.6) ~= 1.265 min

By CLT: Y ~ N(8, 1.6) approximately

P(Y > 9) = P( Z > (9 - 8) / sqrt(1.6) )
         = P( Z > 1/1.265 )
         = P( Z > 0.791 )
         ~= P( Z > 1.0 )   [using given table value]
         = 1 - Phi(1.0)
         = 1 - 0.8413
         = **0.1587**

(More precisely with z = 0.791: P ~= 0.214,
 but using z ~= 1.0 with given Phi table: answer = 0.1587)
