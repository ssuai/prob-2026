Level 3: Advanced/Challenge (Step-by-step Derivation)
Context: Distributed Computing & Straggler Mitigation (Erlang & CLT)

A distributed rendering engine allocates 100 identical image rendering tasks to a pool of worker nodes. Based on continuous-time measurements, the completion of these tasks follows a Poisson process with a rate of λ=4 tasks per minute. Let Y100 be the total time (in minutes) required to complete all 100 tasks.

a) Write the exact integral expression for the probability that the rendering job takes more than 30 minutes to complete (i.e., P(Y100>30)).

b) Since evaluating this integral manually is difficult, use the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) approximation to estimate P(Y100>30). Show your step-by-step derivation for the mean, variance, and the final formula in terms of the standard normal CDF, Φ(x).
