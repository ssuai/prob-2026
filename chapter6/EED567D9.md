# Problem 3 — Hard

## Topic: Competing Exponentials + Erlang PDF + CLT Approximation + Splitting

## Problem Statement

A hospital emergency room has **two types of patients** arriving
independently:

- **Critical patients** arrive as a Poisson process with rate
  $\lambda_c = 2$ per hour.
- **Non-critical patients** arrive as a Poisson process with rate
  $\lambda_n = 6$ per hour.

Each arriving patient (regardless of type) is independently routed to
**Room A** with probability $p = 0.25$ or **Room B** with probability
$1 - p = 0.75$.

Service times in **Room A** are i.i.d. $\text{Exponential}(\mu_A = 4)$
(rate 4 per hour), and in **Room B** are i.i.d.
$\text{Exponential}(\mu_B = 3)$ (rate 3 per hour).

**(a)** At a random moment, both rooms (A and B) are occupied by one patient
each, and you are the next patient assigned to whichever room finishes
first. Find the probability that **you wait more than 15 minutes (0.25 hr)**
before a room becomes free.

**(b)** Find the rate of the merged patient stream arriving at **Room A**,
and compute the expected total service time for the **first 8 patients**
to be served in Room A.

**(c)** You are the **10th patient** to arrive at Room A. Using the
**CLT approximation**, find the probability that the total waiting time
(sum of service times of the first 9 patients ahead of you) **exceeds
2.5 hours**.

**(d)** Suppose a critical patient arrives and finds exactly **3 patients
already waiting** in Room A. What is the probability that **at least
2 of those 3 waiting patients are also critical**?
