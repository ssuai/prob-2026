### Problem 1 — Difficulty: Easy
**Topic**: Geometric PMF, Expectation, Memorylessness  


A telemarketer calls potential customers one by one. Each call independently results in a sale with probability $p = 0.15$. Let $T$ be the number of calls until the first sale (including the successful call).

**(a)** Write down the PMF $p_T(t)$ for $t = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$ and verify it is a valid PMF.

**(b)** Compute $E[T]$ and $\text{Var}(T)$.

**(c)** The telemarketer has already made 8 calls without a sale. What is the probability that the very next call (call 9) results in a sale? 

**(d)** Given that the first 8 calls were all failures, what is the conditional probability that the first sale occurs after call 12?
