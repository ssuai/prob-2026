\## \[Hard] Topic: MAP Estimation in Gaussian Noise



In a communication system, the transmitted signal $\\Theta$ is a random variable that follows a normal distribution with a mean of 0 and a variance of $\\sigma\_0^2 = 4$, i.e., $\\Theta \\sim N(0, 4)$. The received signal $X$ is modeled as the transmitted signal corrupted by Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) $W$, such that $X = \\Theta + W$. The noise $W$ is independent of $\\Theta$ and follows a standard normal distribution, $W \\sim N(0, 1)$.



If the actual observed data at the receiver is $X = 5$, find the \*\*MAP estimate\*\* ($\\hat{\\theta}\_{MAP}$) of the originally transmitted signal $\\Theta$.
