## Problem: Interarrival Times, Pascal PMF, and Bernoulli Process Operations

A Bernoulli process has success probability

\[
p=0.25
\]

in each time slot. Let \(Y_3\) be the time of the third success.

Each success is independently classified as type A with probability

\[
q=0.4,
\]

and type B with probability \(1-q\).

Now suppose there is another independent Bernoulli process with success probability

\[
r=0.15
\]

in each slot.

### Questions

1. What is the PMF of \(Y_3\)?
2. Compute \(P(Y_3=8)\).
3. Find \(E[Y_3]\) and \(\operatorname{var}(Y_3)\).
4. Suppose there have been no successes in the first \(5\) slots. Using the memorylessness property, compute the probability that the first success occurs exactly \(4\) more slots later.

---

### Notes

For the time of the \(k\)-th success,

\[
Y_k=T_1+T_2+\cdots+T_k,
\]

where \(T_1,T_2,\ldots,T_k\) are i.i.d. geometric random variables with parameter \(p\).

The Pascal PMF is

\[
P(Y_k=t)=\binom{t-1}{k-1}p^k(1-p)^{t-k},
\qquad t=k,k+1,\ldots
\]

with

\[
E[Y_k]=\frac{k}{p},
\qquad
\operatorname{var}(Y_k)=\frac{k(1-p)}{p^2}.
\]

For splitting a Bernoulli process:

\[
p_A=pq,
\qquad
p_B=p(1-q).
\]

For merging two independent Bernoulli processes with parameters \(p\) and \(r\):

\[
p_{\text{merged}}=1-(1-p)(1-r)=p+r-pr.
\]
