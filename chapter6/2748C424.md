Context: Network Intrusion Detection Systems (Splitting Processes)

A network intrusion detection system (NIDS) monitors incoming traffic. The arrival of network packets is modeled as a Poisson process with a rate of λ=500 packets per second. The NIDS categorizes each packet independently: it is flagged as 'Suspicious' with probability p=0.05 and 'Benign' with probability 1−p=0.95.

a) Suppose the system administrator observes that exactly 30 'Suspicious' packets arrived in the last second. Calculate the expected number of 'Benign' packets that arrived in that same second. Briefly explain the theoretical property from the lecture that justifies your answer.

b) Let T be the time until the first 'Suspicious' packet arrives. Derive the Probability Density Function (PDF) of T, denoted as fT(t).
