## Problem Statement

A coffee shop receives customer orders via two independent channels:
walk-in customers arrive as a **Poisson process with rate $\lambda_1 = 3$
per hour**, and online orders arrive as a **Poisson process with rate
$\lambda_2 = 5$ per hour**.

Each order (from either channel) is independently classified as
"drink-only" with probability $q = 0.4$, or "drink + food" with probability
$1 - q = 0.6$.

**(a)** What is the probability that **exactly 4 total orders** arrive in
a 30-minute window?

**(b)** Let $D$ be the number of "drink-only" orders in one hour. Find
$E[D]$ and $\text{Var}(D)$.

**(c)** Given that exactly one total order arrived in a 30-minute window,
what is the probability it came from the **walk-in channel**?
