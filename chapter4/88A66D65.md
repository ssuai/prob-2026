## Problem 3: Moment Generating Functions and Convolution
Let $X \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda=2)$ and $Y \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda=3)$ be independent random variables. Define a new random variable $Z = X + Y$.

**Questions:**

(a) Find the Moment Generating Functions (MGF) of $X$ and $Y$ individually ($M_X(s)$ and $M_Y(s)$), then derive $M_Z(s) = M_X(s) \cdot M_Y(s)$. 

Clearly state the valid range of $s$.

(b) Use $M_Z(s)$ to compute $E[Z]$ and $E[Z^2]$, then calculate $\text{Var}(Z)$. 
Compare this result with the sum of individual variances $\text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y)$.

(c) Determine the Probability Density Function (PDF) of $Z$ using the convolution formula:
$$f_Z(z) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_X(x)f_Y(z-x)dx$$
Does the result match your theoretical expectations?
