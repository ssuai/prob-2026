# Problem 1 (Easy)

Let $X$ be uniformly distributed on the interval $[0,2]$, and define

$$
Y=X^2.
$$

Task: Find the probability density function (PDF) of $Y$.

---

# Solution

For $0 \le y \le 4$,

$$
F_Y(y)=P(Y\le y)=P(X^2\le y)=P(X\le \sqrt{y})=\frac{\sqrt{y}}{2}.
$$

Differentiate:

$$
f_Y(y)=\frac{d}{dy}F_Y(y)=\frac{1}{4\sqrt{y}}, \qquad 0<y<4.
$$

Therefore,

$$
f_Y(y)=
\begin{cases}
\dfrac{1}{4\sqrt{y}}, & 0<y<4 \\
0, & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
