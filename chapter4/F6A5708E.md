# Problem 3 (Hard)

Let $N$ be a Poisson random variable with parameter $\lambda>0$. Conditional on $N=n$, let $X_1,\dots,X_n$ be independent exponential random variables with rate $1$, also independent of $N$. Define

$$
S=\sum_{i=1}^{N} X_i,
$$

with the convention $S=0$ when $N=0$.

Task: Find the moment generating function $M_S(t)$.

---

# Solution

Conditional on $N=n$, $S$ is the sum of $n$ independent $\operatorname{Exp}(1)$ variables, so for $t<1$,

$$
M_{S\mid N=n}(t)=\left(\frac{1}{1-t}\right)^n.
$$

Therefore,

$$
M_S(t)=E\big[M_{S\mid N}(t)\big]=E\left[\left(\frac{1}{1-t}\right)^N\right].
$$

Using the Poisson distribution,

$$
M_S(t)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{1-t}\right)^n e^{-\lambda}\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}
= e^{-\lambda} \exp\left(\frac{\lambda}{1-t}\right).
$$

Hence,

$$
M_S(t)=\exp\left(\lambda\left(\frac{1}{1-t}-1\right)\right), \qquad t<1.
$$
