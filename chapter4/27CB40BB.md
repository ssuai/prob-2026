## **Advanced Problem — MGF and Sums of Independent Random Variables (4-4, 4-1)**

### **[A Café Owner’s Monthly Revenue Analysis]**

Suppose the daily revenue of a café (measured in units of **10,000 KRW**) is denoted by (X_i). Assume that each day’s revenue follows a normal distribution:

[
X_i \sim N(80,25^2)
]

and that revenues across different business days are **independent**.

Let the cumulative revenue over 30 days be defined as

[
S=X_1+X_2+\cdots+X_{30}
]

Analyze the distribution of the total monthly revenue.

### **(a)**

Given that the moment generating function (MGF) of a normal random variable is

M_{X_i}(s)=\exp!\left(80s+\frac{625}{2}s^2\right)

use the **multiplicative property of MGFs for independent random variables** to derive

[
M_S(s)
]

and determine the distribution of (S), including its **mean** and **variance**.

### **(b)**

Express the probability that the cumulative 30-day revenue exceeds **25,000,000 KRW** (i.e., 2,500 in units of 10,000 KRW),

[
P(S\ge2500)
]

in terms of the **standard normal cumulative distribution function (CDF)**

[
\Phi
]

### **(c)**

Suppose the café owner also incurs a daily advertising cost, denoted by (C_i), independently distributed as

[
C_i\sim N(5,2^2)
]

(in units of **10,000 KRW**) and independent of (X_i).

Define the total **net profit** over 30 days as

[
T=\sum_{i=1}^{30}(X_i-C_i)
]

Find the MGF of (T) and derive the probability distribution of (T).

### **(d) Conceptual Interpretation**

From the perspective of **moment generating functions (MGFs)**, explain intuitively **why the sum of normal random variables is again normally distributed**.

How is this property different from the sum of independent random variables from other distributions (for example, the uniform distribution)? What makes the normal distribution special in this context?
