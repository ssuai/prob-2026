## Problem: Covariance, Correlation, and Dependence

Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be discrete random variables with the following joint PMF.

| \(p_{X,Y}(x,y)\) | \(Y=0\) | \(Y=1\) | \(Y=2\) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| \(X=0\) | \(1/8\) | \(1/8\) | \(0\) |
| \(X=1\) | \(1/8\) | \(1/4\) | \(1/8\) |
| \(X=2\) | \(0\) | \(1/8\) | \(1/8\) |

Answer the following questions.

### Questions

1. Find the marginal PMFs \(p_X(x)\) and \(p_Y(y)\).
2. Compute \(E[X]\), \(E[Y]\), \(\operatorname{var}(X)\), \(\operatorname{var}(Y)\), and \(E[XY]\).
3. Compute

\[
\operatorname{cov}(X,Y)
\]

and the correlation coefficient

\[
\rho(X,Y)=\frac{\operatorname{cov}(X,Y)}
{\sqrt{\operatorname{var}(X)\operatorname{var}(Y)}}.
\]

---

### Notes

You may use

\[
\operatorname{cov}(X,Y)=E[XY]-E[X]E[Y]
\]

and

\[
\operatorname{var}(X+Y)=\operatorname{var}(X)+\operatorname{var}(Y)+2\operatorname{cov}(X,Y).
\]
