# Problem 1 (Easy): Covariance and Correlation

Two discrete random variables $X$ and $Y$ have the following joint PMF.

|  | $Y = 0$ | $Y = 2$ |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| $X = 0$ | $\dfrac{1}{3}$ | $\dfrac{1}{6}$ |
| $X = 1$ | $\dfrac{1}{6}$ | $\dfrac{1}{3}$ |

Using the marginal distributions, find $E[X]$, $E[Y]$, and $E[XY]$. Then compute the covariance $\text{cov}(X, Y) = E[XY] - E[X]E[Y]$ and the correlation coefficient $\rho(X, Y) = \text{cov}(X, Y) / \sqrt{\text{var}(X)\,\text{var}(Y)}$. Finally, determine whether $X$ and $Y$ are independent by comparing a specific joint probability with the product of the corresponding marginal probabilities.
