# Problem 2 (Intermediate)

A random variable $X$ takes the values $-1$ and $1$ with equal probability. Given $X=x$, another random variable $Y$ is defined by

- $Y=x$ with probability $\tfrac{3}{4}$,
- $Y=-x$ with probability $\tfrac{1}{4}$.

Task: Find $\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)$.

---

# Solution

By symmetry,

$$
E[X]=0, \qquad E[Y]=0.
$$

Also, $XY=1$ when $Y=X$ and $XY=-1$ when $Y=-X$, so

$$
E[XY]=1\cdot \frac{3}{4}+(-1)\cdot \frac{1}{4}=\frac{1}{2}.
$$

Hence,

$$
\operatorname{Cov}(X,Y)=E[XY]-E[X]E[Y]=\frac{1}{2}.
$$
