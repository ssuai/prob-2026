# Hard Problem: Difference in Arrival Times

Two delivery drivers are assigned to arrive at the same apartment complex sometime between **9:00 AM and 10:00 AM**. Because of traffic, parking, and route differences, their exact arrival times are random.

Let \(X\) be the arrival time of the first driver, measured in hours after 9:00 AM.  
Let \(Y\) be the arrival time of the second driver, measured in hours after 9:00 AM.

Assume that \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent random variables, and each is uniformly distributed on \([0,1]\).

Define a new random variable

\[
Z=|X-Y|
\]

where \(Z\) represents the absolute difference between the two drivers’ arrival times.

---

## Questions

**(a)** Determine the possible range of \(Z\).

**(b)** Find the cumulative distribution function of \(Z\),

\[
F_Z(z)=P(Z\le z).
\]

**(c)** Find the probability density function of \(Z\),

\[
f_Z(z),
\]

by differentiating the CDF.

**(d)** Use your result to find the probability that the difference between the two arrival times is more than 15 minutes.
