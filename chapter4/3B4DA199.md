# Intermediate Problem: Covariance and Error Rates

A communication system transmits two binary signals, \(X\) and \(Y\), during the same time interval.

- \(X = 1\) means the first signal is received incorrectly.
- \(Y = 1\) means the second signal is received incorrectly.

Suppose the joint probabilities are given as follows:

| \(X\) | \(Y\) | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0 | 0.72 |
| 1 | 0 | 0.08 |
| 0 | 1 | 0.12 |
| 1 | 1 | 0.08 |

1. Find \(E[X]\) and \(E[Y]\).

2. Compute the covariance

\[
\operatorname{cov}(X,Y).
\]

3. Are the two error events positively correlated, negatively correlated, or uncorrelated? Explain your reasoning.

4. Let

\[
T = X + Y
\]

be the total number of transmission errors during the interval.

Find

\[
\operatorname{var}(T).
\]

(Hint: Use the covariance formula for the variance of a sum.)
