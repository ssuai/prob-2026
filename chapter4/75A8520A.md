# Intermediate Problem

A university is analyzing the relationship between students’ study habits and exam performance.

For a randomly selected student,

- X represents the number of hours spent studying for a statistics exam,
- Y represents the exam score improvement after attending a tutoring session.

The university defines the following joint distribution:

(X, Y) ∈ {(1,2), (2,4), (3,6), (4,8)}

and each outcome occurs with probability 1/4.

The research team wants to understand whether study time and score improvement are positively related.

1. Find E[X] and E[Y].
2. Compute cov(X,Y).
3. Determine whether X and Y are positively correlated.
