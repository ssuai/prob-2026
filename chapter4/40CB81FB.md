## 3. Hard: Covariance & Indicator Random Variables
Style of Lecture Material 4-2 (Example 4.15 - Phone Picking)

Suppose $n$ students put their bags in one place and then randomly pick them up one by one. Let $X$ be the number of students who correctly select their own bag. Describe the process of finding $var(X)$.

(Note: Define $X_i$ as an indicator random variable that is 1 if the $i$-th student finds their own bag and 0 otherwise, and use the fact that $X = \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$)
