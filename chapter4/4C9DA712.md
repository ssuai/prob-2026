# Problem 2 (Intermediate): Moment Generating Function

The moment generating function (MGF) of a random variable $X$ is given by

$$M_X(s) = \frac{1}{4}e^{-2s} + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4}e^{4s}.$$

By recognizing the pattern of the MGF of a discrete random variable, $M_X(s) = \sum_{x} e^{sx} p_X(x)$, identify the PMF $p_X(x)$. Then differentiate $M_X(s)$ once and twice with respect to $s$ and evaluate at $s = 0$ to find $E[X]$ and $E[X^2]$, respectively. Use these results to compute $\text{Var}(X)$.
