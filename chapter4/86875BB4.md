# Problem (Hard) — The Hat Problem

$n$ people throw their hats into a box and each person randomly
picks one hat. Let $X_i$ be the indicator random variable such that:

$$X_i = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if person } i \text{ picks their own hat} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

Let $X = X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n$ be the total number of people
who pick their own hat.

**(a)** What distribution does each $X_i$ follow?
Find $E[X_i]$ and $\text{var}(X_i)$.

**(b)** For $i \neq j$, find $\text{cov}(X_i, X_j)$.

> **Hint:** Use $\text{cov}(X_i, X_j) = E[X_iX_j] - E[X_i]E[X_j]$
> and think carefully about $P(X_i = 1 \text{ and } X_j = 1)$.

**(c)** Using the variance of sum formula, compute $\text{var}(X)$.

$$\text{var}(X) = \sum_{i=1}^n \text{var}(X_i) + 2\sum_{i<j} \text{cov}(X_i, X_j)$$

**(d)** Are $X_i$ and $X_j$ independent for $i \neq j$?
Justify your answer using the definition of independence.
