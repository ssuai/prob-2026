## 2. Intermediate: Conditional Expectation and Variance of Sums
A sensor network is collecting two data streams, $X$ and $Y$. The variances of each stream are known to be $var(X) = 4$ and $var(Y) = 9$. The correlation between the two streams is represented by the covariance $cov(X, Y) = 2$.

(a) Calculate the variance of the combined signal $S = 2X + Y$.
(Hint: Recall the general rule learned in class: $var(aX + bY) = a^2 var(X) + b^2 var(Y) + 2ab \cdot cov(X, Y)$)

(b) Assume there is a new random variable $Z$ used to estimate $X$. If the conditional expectation $E[X|Z]$ is an unbiased estimator of $X$ (i.e., it satisfies $E[E[X|Z]] = E[X]$), prove that the mean of the estimation error $\tilde{X} = E[X|Z] - X$ is exactly 0.

(c) If $X$ and $Y$ are uncorrelated ($cov(X, Y) = 0$), how would the variance of $S = 2X + Y$ change? Calculate the new value and explain the physical meaning of positive (+) covariance in terms of signal fluctuation.
