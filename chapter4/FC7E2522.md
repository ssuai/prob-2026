## Problem: Conditional Expectation with a Random Coin Bias

Let \(Y\) be a random variable uniformly distributed on \([0,1]\). After observing \(Y=y\), a coin with probability of heads \(y\) is tossed independently \(4\) times.

Let \(X\) be the number of heads in the \(4\) tosses. Thus,

\[
X\mid Y=y \sim \operatorname{Binomial}(4,y).
\]

Define the conditional expectation estimator

\[
\widehat{X}=E[X\mid Y].
\]

Answer the following questions.

### Questions

1. Find \(E[X\mid Y]\) and \(\operatorname{var}(X\mid Y)\).
2. Compute \(E[X]\).
3. Compute \(\operatorname{var}(\widehat{X})\).
4. Compute

\[
E[\operatorname{var}(X\mid Y)].
\]

5. Use the law of total variance to compute \(\operatorname{var}(X)\):

\[
\operatorname{var}(X)
=
E[\operatorname{var}(X\mid Y)]
+
\operatorname{var}(E[X\mid Y]).
\]

---

### Notes

You may use the following identities:

\[
E[X\mid Y=y]=4y,
\]

\[
\operatorname{var}(X\mid Y=y)=4y(1-y),
\]

and

\[
P(X=k)=\int_0^1 \binom{4}{k}y^k(1-y)^{4-k}\,dy.
\]
