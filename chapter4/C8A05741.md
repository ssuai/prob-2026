**Problem 3: Hierarchical Processing, Covariance, and Continuous Inference**
A continuous manufacturing system processes a component in two dependent stages. The time required for the first stage is a random variable $X$ which follows an exponential distribution with rate parameter $\lambda > 0$. The setup of the second stage is entirely dependent on the duration of the first stage: given that the first stage takes $X = x$ hours, the time required for the second stage, $Y$, is exponentially distributed with rate parameter $x$ (i.e., $Y | X=x \sim \text{Exp}(x)$). 

**Part A (Covariance and Interaction):** 
Determine whether $X$ and $Y$ are positively correlated, negatively correlated, or uncorrelated. Prove your claim by analytically computing the covariance, $\text{cov}(X,Y)$. 
*(Hint: Use conditional expectation properties to find $E[Y]$ and $E[XY]$).*

**Part B (Bayesian Inversion and Conditional Expectation):** 
Suppose an observer only records the duration of the second stage, $Y = y$. Derive the conditional PDF of $X$ given $Y = y$, $f_{X|Y}(x|y)$, and compute the best estimator for the first stage duration, $\hat{X} = E[X|Y=y]$. 

**Part C (Transformations):**
Let $U = X$ and $V = XY$. Find the joint probability density function $f_{U,V}(u,v)$ and use it to determine the marginal PDF of $V$.
