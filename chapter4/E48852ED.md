# Intermediate Problem
 
A company evaluates an employee using three performance scores:
 
$$X = \text{technical score},$$
 
$$Y = \text{communication score},$$
 
$$W = \text{teamwork score}.$$
 
The final evaluation score is defined as
 
$$Z = 2X - Y + 3W.$$
 
Suppose
 
$$E[X] = 70, \quad E[Y] = 60, \quad E[W] = 80,$$
 
$$\text{Var}(X) = 25, \quad \text{Var}(Y) = 16, \quad \text{Var}(W) = 36,$$
 
and
 
$$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = 6, \quad \text{Cov}(X,W) = -4, \quad \text{Cov}(Y,W) = 10.$$
 
Find $E[Z]$ and $\text{Var}(Z)$.
 
Then determine whether the covariance terms **increase or decrease** $\text{Var}(Z)$ compared with the case where $X$, $Y$, and $W$ are uncorrelated.
