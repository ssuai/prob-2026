**Problem 2: Conditional Expectation and Variance Decomposition**
A retail store sees a random number of customers, $N$, enter per hour, where $N$ follows a Poisson distribution with parameter $\lambda$. Each customer independently decides to make a purchase with probability $p$. Let $X_i$ be an indicator random variable that equals $1$ if the $i$-th customer makes a purchase, and $0$ otherwise. The total number of purchases is $Y = \sum_{i=1}^N X_i$. Assume $N$ and the sequence $X_i$ are independent.

**Part A:** Compute the conditional expectation $E[Y|N]$ and use the Law of Total Expectation to find the unconditional expectation $E[Y]$.
**Part B:** Compute the conditional variance $\text{var}(Y|N)$.
**Part C:** Use the Law of Total Variance (Variance Decomposition Formula), $\text{var}(Y) = E[\text{var}(Y|N)] + \text{var}(E[Y|N])$, to find the unconditional variance of $Y$.
