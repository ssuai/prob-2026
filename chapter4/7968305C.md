## **Intermediate Problem — Conditional Expectation and Covariance (4-2, 4-3)**

### **[The Relationship Between Coffee Consumption and Study Time at a Café]**

A student analyzed their study habits while working in a café. Let the number of cups of coffee consumed, denoted by (Y), follow a discrete uniform distribution over the set

[
{1,2,3,4}
]

That is, each value occurs with equal probability.

The amount of study time, denoted by (X) (in hours), depends on the number of cups of coffee consumed. Specifically, conditional on (Y=y), suppose that

[
X \mid Y=y \sim U[0,2y]
]

meaning that the study time is uniformly distributed between 0 and (2y) hours (approximately one hour of average study time per cup of coffee).

### **(a)**

Express

[
E[X \mid Y=y]
]

as a function of (y), and then use the **Law of Iterated Expectations** to calculate

[
E[X]
]

### **(b)**

Compute

[
E[XY]
]

and use it to determine the covariance

[
\mathrm{cov}(X,Y)
]

### **(c)**

Find the correlation coefficient

[
\rho(X,Y)
]


### **Hint**

When computing

[
\mathrm{Var}(X)
]

use the **Total Variance Theorem**:

\mathrm{Var}(X)=E[\mathrm{Var}(X\mid Y)]+\mathrm{Var}(E[X\mid Y])
