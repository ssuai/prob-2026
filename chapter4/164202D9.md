# Problem 3 — A Gaussian Channel: MGFs, Covariance, and Estimation

**Difficulty:** Hard

---

## Problem Statement

A signal $X$ is transmitted through a noisy channel and received as $Y$:

$$
X \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1), \qquad W \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2), \qquad Y = X + W,
$$

with $X$ and $W$ independent and $\sigma^2 > 0$ known.

You may use the fact that the MGF of $\mathcal{N}(\mu, \tau^2)$ is $e^{\mu s + \tau^2 s^2 / 2}$.

### Sub-parts

**(a) Distribution of $Y$ via MGFs.**
Compute $M_X(s)$, $M_W(s)$, and $M_Y(s)$. Then identify the distribution of $Y$ by pattern matching, and write down $E[Y]$ and $\mathrm{var}(Y)$.

**(b) Covariance and correlation.**
Compute $\mathrm{cov}(X, Y)$ and $\rho(X, Y)$. Then evaluate $\rho(X, Y)$ in the two limits $\sigma \to 0$ and $\sigma \to \infty$.

**(c) Conditional expectation as estimator.**
For this Gaussian channel, the conditional expectation estimator is given by
$$
\hat{X} = E[X \mid Y] = \frac{Y}{1 + \sigma^2}.
$$
*(You may use this formula without derivation.)* Compute $E[\hat{X}]$.

**(d) Variances and covariance of the estimator.**
Let $\tilde{X} = X - \hat{X}$ be the estimation error.

  (i) Compute $\mathrm{var}(\hat{X})$.

  (ii) Compute $\mathrm{var}(\tilde{X})$ using $\mathrm{var}(\tilde{X}) = E[\mathrm{var}(X \mid Y)]$, given that $\mathrm{var}(X \mid Y) = \dfrac{\sigma^2}{1 + \sigma^2}$ is constant in $Y$.

  (iii) Compute $\mathrm{cov}(\hat{X}, \tilde{X})$.

**(e) Numerical evaluation at $\sigma^2 = 3$.**
Compute the numerical values of: $\mathrm{var}(Y)$, $\rho(X, Y)$, $\mathrm{var}(\hat{X})$, $\mathrm{var}(\tilde{X})$, and the sum $\mathrm{var}(\hat{X}) + \mathrm{var}(\tilde{X})$.
