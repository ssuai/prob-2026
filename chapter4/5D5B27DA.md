# Exercise 1: Covariance and Variance of Sums
**Level:** Easy
**Objective:** Apply the general rule for the variance of the sum of two random variables.

## Problem
Let $X$ and $Y$ be two dependent random variables. You are given the following information:
* $var(X) = 5$
* $var(Y) = 8$
* $cov(X, Y) = -3$

Calculate the variance of their sum, $var(X + Y)$.

---

## Solution
To find the variance of the sum of two dependent random variables, we use the general formula:
1. **Formula:** $var(X + Y) = var(X) + var(Y) + 2cov(X, Y)$.
2. **Substitution:** Substitute the given values into the equation:
   $$var(X + Y) = 5 + 8 + 2(-3)$$
3. **Calculation:** $$var(X + Y) = 13 - 6 = 7$$
