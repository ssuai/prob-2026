# Intermediate Problem: Study Goal and Actual Completion

A student sets a study goal for the day and then records how much of that goal they actually complete.

Let \(Y\) be the **planned study portion**, and let \(X\) be the **actual completed portion**.

Both \(X\) and \(Y\) are measured as proportions of the full daily study target, so their values are between 0 and 1.

The student cannot complete more than the planned amount, so the possible values satisfy

\[
0<X<Y<1.
\]

Assume that the pair \((X,Y)\) is uniformly distributed over the region

\[
0<x<y<1.
\]

---

## Questions

**(a)** Find the joint pdf \(f_{X,Y}(x,y)\).

**(b)** Use marginalization to find the marginal pdf \(f_Y(y)\).

**(c)** Find the conditional pdf \(f_{X|Y}(x|y)\).

**(d)** Calculate

\[
E[X|Y=y].
\]

**(e)** Let

\[
\hat X=E[X|Y].
\]

Express \(\hat X\) as a function of \(Y\).
