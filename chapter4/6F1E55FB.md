# Problem 2: Estimating Component Lifespan

**Problem Statement:**
Suppose you are a quality control engineer trying to estimate the true lifespan of a particular electronic component, denoted by the continuous random variable $X$. From historical data, you know that $X$ is uniformly distributed between 0 and 1 (measured in thousands of hours).

You cannot measure the total lifespan $X$ directly. Instead, you take a quick diagnostic measurement, $Y$. Because the diagnostic tool is noisy and depends on the component's true lifespan, the measurement $Y$ is uniformly distributed between $0$ and the true lifespan $X$.

**Your Task:**
Find the best estimate of the component's lifespan $X$ given an observed diagnostic measurement $Y = y$. (Note: The "best estimate" in terms of Minimum Mean Square Error is the conditional expectation $E[X|Y=y]$).
