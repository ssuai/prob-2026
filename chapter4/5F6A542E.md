In engineering problems, we often need to estimate an original quantity $X$ by observing an indirect measurement $Y$. Let the conditional expectation estimator be defined as $\hat{X} = E[X|Y]$, and the estimation error as $\tilde{X} = \hat{X} - X$.  

1. Using the properties of expectation, prove that this estimator $\hat{X}$ is an unbiased estimator.

2. Derive the mathematical relationship between the variance of the estimation error $var(\tilde{X})$, the variance of the original variable $var(X)$, and the variance of the estimator $var(\hat{X})$.
