# Problem 1 — Easy

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Topic:** Chebyshev Inequality

---

## Problem Statement

Let $X$ be a random variable with mean $\mu = 10$ and variance $\sigma^2 = 4$.

**(a)** Using the Chebyshev inequality, find an upper bound for the following probability:

$$P(|X - 10| \geq 4)$$

**(b)** Using the alternative form of the Chebyshev inequality $P(|X - \mu| \geq k\sigma) \leq \dfrac{1}{k^2}$, express the bound in part (a) in terms of $k$ and verify your answer matches part (a).

**(c)** Suppose $X \sim \text{Uniform}[6, 14]$. Compute the **exact** probability $P(|X - 10| \geq 4)$ and compare it with the Chebyshev bound. Is the Chebyshev bound tight in this case?
