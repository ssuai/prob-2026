# Hard Difficulty Problem: Estimating Average Daily Screen Time

A university wants to estimate the true mean daily screen time \(\mu\), measured in hours, of its students. Since it is impossible to ask every student, the university randomly selects \(n\) students independently and records their daily screen times:

\[
X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n
\]

Assume that \(X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n\) are i.i.d. random variables with

\[
E[X_i]=\mu
\]

and

\[
SD(X_i)=3 \text{ hours}.
\]

The university uses the sample mean

\[
M_n=\frac{X_1+X_2+\cdots+X_n}{n}
\]

as an estimate of the true mean \(\mu\).

Answer the following questions.

---

## Problem

**(a)** Find

\[
E[M_n], \quad Var(M_n), \quad SD(M_n).
\]

What does \(E[M_n]\) imply about \(M_n\) as an estimator of the true mean \(\mu\)?

---

**(b)** How large should \(n\) be so that the standard deviation of \(M_n\) is at most \(0.1\) hours?

---

**(c)** Using Chebyshev’s inequality, how large should \(n\) be to guarantee that the sample mean \(M_n\) is within \(0.25\) hours of the true mean \(\mu\) with probability at least \(0.96\)?

---

**(d)** Due to budget limitations, the university can survey only \(1000\) students. Using Chebyshev’s inequality, find a lower bound for the probability that the sample mean \(M_{1000}\) is within \(0.25\) hours of the true mean \(\mu\).

Based on this bound, can the university guarantee that its estimate is within \(0.25\) hours with probability at least \(0.96\)?

---

**(e)** If the university samples \(2500\) students, find the smallest error margin \(c\), measured in **hours**, such that Chebyshev’s inequality guarantees that the sample mean is within \(c\) hours of the true mean \(\mu\) with probability at least \(0.96\).

That is, find the smallest \(c\) satisfying

\[
P(|M_{2500}-\mu|<c)\ge 0.96.
\]
