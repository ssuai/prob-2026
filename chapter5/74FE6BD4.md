# **Problem 1: Packet Processing Deviations (Chebyshev Inequality - Easy)**



###### **\[Condition]**

###### To support emerging 400G optical networking speeds, a major telecom provider is utilizing a high-performance software router running on commodity processors that offloads stateful packet processing to an internal pipeline. To prevent internal queue buffer overflows and cascading network failures, the variability of the packet processing time must be tightly constrained. Telemetry indicates that the mean packet processing time within the stateful data plane is $\\mu = 45$ microseconds ($\\mu s$), with a known, proven variance of $\\sigma^2 = 16 \\mu s^2$.

###### **\[Problem]**

###### Using Chebyshev's Inequality, calculate the maximum probability that a specific packet's processing time will deviate from the average by $20 \\mu s$ or more (i.e., completing exceptionally fast in under $25 \\mu s$, indicating dropped state logic, or lagging to over $65 \\mu s$, risking a buffer overflow).
