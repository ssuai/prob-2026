# Easy Problem 

Let \(X\) be a nonnegative random variable representing the operating time (in hours) of a portable battery pack. Suppose that

\[
E[X] = 12
\]

Use the Markov inequality to find an upper bound for the probability that the battery pack lasts at least 18 hours.

\[
P(X \ge 18)
\]

Then briefly explain why the result obtained from the Markov inequality may not be equal to the actual probability.
