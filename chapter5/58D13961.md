## Problem 1: Basic Tail Bounds
Let $X$ be a random variable representing the daily revenue (in millions of KRW) of a restaurant near a university. Suppose the expected daily revenue is 2 million KRW with a standard deviation of 0.3 million KRW. Assume that the revenue is always non-negative ($X \ge 0$).

1. Use **Markov's inequality** to find an upper bound on the probability that the daily revenue is at least 3 million KRW, i.e., $P(X \ge 3)$.
2. Use **Chebyshev's inequality** to find an upper bound on the probability that the daily revenue is either less than or equal to 1.1 million KRW or greater than or equal to 2.9 million KRW, i.e., $P(|X - 2| \ge 0.9)$.
