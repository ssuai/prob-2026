## Problem: Weak Law of Large Numbers

Let \(X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n\) be i.i.d. random variables with

\[
E[X_i]=50,\qquad \operatorname{var}(X_i)=25.
\]

Define the sample mean

\[
M_n=\frac{X_1+X_2+\cdots+X_n}{n}.
\]

Answer the following questions.

### Questions

1. Find \(E[M_n]\).
2. Find \(\operatorname{var}(M_n)\).
3. Using Chebyshev's inequality, find an upper bound for

\[
P(|M_n-50|\ge 2).
\]

4. Find the smallest integer \(n\) that guarantees

\[
P(|M_n-50|\ge 2)\le 0.01
\]

using the Chebyshev bound.

---

### Notes

You may use

\[
\operatorname{var}(M_n)=\frac{\sigma^2}{n}
\]

for i.i.d. random variables, and

\[
P(|M_n-\mu|\ge \epsilon)\le \frac{\sigma^2}{n\epsilon^2}.
\]
