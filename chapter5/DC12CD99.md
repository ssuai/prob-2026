# Problem 2: Sample Size Determination for Chess AI Evaluation

**Problem Statement:**
You are evaluating a new heuristic for a chess AI. Let $X_i$ be the time (in milliseconds) it takes for the engine to evaluate the $i$-th board position. Assume that the evaluation times across different positions are independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) random variables. 

The true average evaluation time, $\mu = E[X_i]$, is unknown, but from prior architectural testing, you know the variance is $\sigma^2 = \text{Var}(X_i) = 36 \text{ ms}^2$. To estimate $\mu$, you run the engine on $n$ different positions and calculate the sample mean:
$$\bar{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i$$

**Your Task:**
Using the bound provided by Chebyshev's inequality (which forms the basis of the Weak Law of Large Numbers), find the minimum number of board positions $n$ you must evaluate to guarantee that the probability of your sample mean $\bar{X}_n$ being within $2 \text{ ms}$ of the true mean $\mu$ is at least $90\%$, i.e., $P(|\bar{X}_n - \mu| < 2) \ge 0.90$.
