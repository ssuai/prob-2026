# Problem 1 (Easy)

In a custom dynamic array (`VArray`) implemented in Java, the time taken for a standard `add` operation, denoted as $X$, was measured. Due to the variability of the system environment, the exact probability density function (PDF) of $X$ is unknown. However, it is known that the mean is $10\mu s$ and the variance is $4(\mu s)^2$.

Using Chebyshev's Inequality, find the maximum possible probability (upper bound) that the `add` operation time deviates from its mean by $4\mu s$ or more.
