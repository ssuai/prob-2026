## Problem 3: Central Limit Theorem and Distribution Approximation
An airplane is loaded with 100 independent pieces of luggage. The weight of each luggage, denoted as $X_i$ (for $i = 1, 2, \dots, 100$), is independently and uniformly distributed between $10\text{ kg}$ and $40\text{ kg}$, such that $X_i \sim \text{Unif}([10, 40])$.

1. Find the expected value $E[X_i]$ and the variance $\text{var}(X_i)$ for a single piece of luggage.
2. Let $S_{100} = \sum_{i=1}^{100} X_i$ be the total weight of the 100 pieces of luggage. Use the **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)** to approximate the probability that the total weight exceeds $2600\text{ kg}$, i.e., $P(S_{100} > 2600)$. Express your answer using the standard normal CDF, $\Phi(z)$. 
*(Note: Round the standard normal $z$-score to two decimal places when finding the final expression.)*
