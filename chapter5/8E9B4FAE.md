## Problem 2: Sample Mean and the Weak Law of Large Numbers
The lifetime $X$ of an electronic component produced by a factory has a mean of $\mu = 1000$ hours and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 100$ hours. A quality control engineer randomly selects $n$ independent components and measures their sample mean, denoted as $M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \dots + X_n}{n}$.

1. Using the **Chebyshev inequality** (as applied in the proof of the Weak Law of Large Numbers), derive an upper bound in terms of $n$ for the probability that the sample mean deviates from the true mean by 10 hours or more, i.e., $P(|M_n - 1000| \ge 10)$.
2. Determine the minimum sample size $n$ required to guarantee that this upper bound is at most $0.05$.
