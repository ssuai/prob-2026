# Exercise 2: Sample Size and the Weak Law of Large Numbers
**Level:** Intermediate

## Problem
You are estimating the mean $\mu$ of a population using the sample mean $M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \dots + X_n}{n}$, where each $X_i$ is independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) with variance $\sigma^2 = 2$.

Based on the **Weak Law of Large Numbers** (specifically the Chebyshev bound for $M_n$), determine the minimum number of measurements $n$ required to ensure that the probability of the sample mean differing from the true mean by 0.5 or more is at most 0.1.

In other words, find $n$ such that:
$$P(|M_n - \mu| \ge 0.5) \le 0.1$$
