**Problem 4: Sample Mean Convergence and Chebyshev**
An environmental scientist is measuring the concentration of a pollutant in a large lake. The concentration from a single water sample, $X_i$, is a random variable with an unknown true mean $\mu$ and a variance of $\sigma^2 = 2.5$. The scientist takes $n = 100$ independent samples and computes the sample mean $M_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$.
(a) Use Chebyshev's inequality to bound the probability that the sample mean $M_n$ deviates from the true mean $\mu$ by more than $0.5$ units.
(b) Interpret this result in the context of the Weak Law of Large Numbers. If the scientist wants the bound on this probability to be less than or equal to $0.01$, what is the minimum sample size $n$ required?
