**Problem 5: Bounding Empirical Frequencies without Prior Parameters**
A randomized algorithm outputs a correct classification with an unknown probability $p$. To estimate $p$, you run the algorithm $n$ independent times and record the indicator variables $X_i \in \{0, 1\}$. You define the empirical frequency as $M_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$.
(a) You require that the empirical frequency $M_n$ is within $0.04$ of the true probability $p$ with a probability of at least $0.95$. Since $p$ is unknown, you cannot directly compute $Var(X_i)$. Determine the theoretical maximum for $Var(X_i)$ and use this to find the minimum number of independent trials $n$ required to satisfy this guarantee using Chebyshev's inequality.
(b) Discuss the tightness of this sample size bound. If the true probability was actually $p = 0.01$, how conservative is the sample size derived in part (a) compared to the actual variance?
