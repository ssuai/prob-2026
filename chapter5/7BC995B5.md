## Problem 3 (Hard)

A quantitative researcher is analyzing a sequence of independent and identically distributed random variables $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n$, representing the daily fluctuations of a highly volatile financial asset, where each fluctuation is known to have a mean $\mu = 0$ and a finite, positive variance $\sigma^2$. To estimate the volatility, the researcher defines the variance estimator as $V_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n X_i^2$, and wishes to guarantee that $V_n$ falls within $10\%$ of the true variance $\sigma^2$ with a probability of at least $0.95$. Assuming that the fourth moment of the fluctuations is $E[X_i^4] = 3\sigma^4$, rigorously demonstrate that $V_n$ converges in probability to $\sigma^2$ as $n \to \infty$ by invoking the Weak Law of Large Numbers, and then mathematically apply the Central Limit Theorem to the sequence of transformed variables $Y_i = X_i^2$ to derive the exact minimum sample size $n$ required to satisfy the researcher's precision requirement (leaving your final expression for $n$ in terms of the standard normal quantile $z_{0.025}$).
