## Problem 2 

A machine learning model produces prediction errors $E = Y - \hat{Y}$ with mean $E[E] = 0$ and variance $\text{var}(E) = \sigma^2$.

Two improvement strategies are proposed:

- **Strategy A**: Reduce the variance to $\sigma^2/4$ while keeping the mean at 0.
- **Strategy B**: Reduce the variance to $\sigma^2/2$, but introduce a bias $\mu_B \neq 0$.

**(a)** After applying Strategy A, by what factor does the Chebyshev upper bound on $P(|E| \geq c)$ decrease compared to the original?

**(b)** We require $P(|E| \geq c) \leq \alpha$. Using the Chebyshev inequality, find the maximum allowable value of $|\mu_B|$ under Strategy B. Express your answer in terms of $\sigma$, $c$, and $\alpha$.

**(c)** For $c = 3\sigma$ and $\alpha = 0.1$, compute the maximum allowable bias $|\mu_B|$ from part (b).
