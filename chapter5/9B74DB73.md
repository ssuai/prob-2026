# Problem 2 — Intermediate

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Topic:** Weak Law of Large Numbers + Chebyshev Inequality

---

## Problem Statement

A fair six-sided die is rolled $n$ times independently. Let $X_i$ be the outcome of the $i$-th roll, and let $M_n = \dfrac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}$ be the sample mean.

Recall: For a discrete uniform distribution on $\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}$,

$$E[X_i] = 3.5, \qquad \text{var}(X_i) = \frac{35}{12}$$

**(a)** Using the Chebyshev inequality, find an upper bound for $P(|M_n - 3.5| \geq 0.5)$ in terms of $n$.

**(b)** Using your result from (a), determine the minimum number of rolls $n$ required so that:

$$P(|M_n - 3.5| \geq 0.5) \leq 0.01$$

**(c)** As $n \to \infty$, what does the Weak Law of Large Numbers say about $M_n$? State the conclusion precisely using the $\epsilon$-$\delta$ limit notation.
