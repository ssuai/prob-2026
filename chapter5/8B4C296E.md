**[Easy Problem: Basic Bound Analysis]**
The number of network packets, X, arriving at a router's 
inbound port per millisecond is a non-negative random 
variable. Traffic logs show that the average arrival rate 
is E[X] = 120 packets/ms. 

The router's buffer can safely handle a maximum of 600 
packets/ms. Using Markov's inequality, calculate the strict 
upper bound on the probability that the buffer overflows 
in any given millisecond.
