# Easy Difficulty Problem: The Over-Caffeinated Student

A student is trying to survive the semester with coffee. Let \(X\) be the number of cups of coffee the student drinks in one day.

Assume that \(X\) is a nonnegative random variable and

\[
E[X]=2.5.
\]

Answer the following questions using Markov’s inequality.

---

## Problem

**(a)** Explain why Markov’s inequality can be applied to \(X\).

**(b)** Use Markov’s inequality to find an upper bound for the probability that the student drinks at least \(8\) cups of coffee in one day.

That is, find an upper bound for

\[
P(X\ge 8).
\]

**(c)** Find the smallest value of \(a\) such that Markov’s inequality guarantees

\[
P(X\ge a)\le 0.1.
\]
