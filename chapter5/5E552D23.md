# Problem 1 (Easy)

**Topic: Markov Inequality & Chebyshev Inequality**

A server's response time $X$ (ms) is a non-negative random variable with:

$$E[X] = 6 \text{ ms}, \qquad \text{var}(X) = 9$$

The operations team wants to bound the probability of unusually slow responses without knowing the full distribution of $X$.

**(a)** [Markov Inequality] Apply the Markov inequality to find an upper bound on $P(X \geq 12)$.

$$P(X \geq a) \leq \frac{E[X]}{a}$$

**(b)** [Chebyshev Inequality] Apply the Chebyshev inequality to find an upper bound on $P(|X - 6| \geq 6)$.

$$P(|X - \mu| \geq c) \leq \frac{\text{var}(X)}{c^2}$$

**(c)** [Alternative form] Express the Chebyshev bound from (b) using the $k\sigma$ form:

$$P(|X - \mu| \geq k\sigma) \leq \frac{1}{k^2}$$

Identify the value of $k$ and verify it gives the same bound.

**(d)** [Comparison] The team observes empirically that $P(X \geq 12) = 0.02$. Compare this with the Markov bound from (a). Is the Markov bound tight in this case?
