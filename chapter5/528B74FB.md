# Problem 2 (Intermediate)

Let $X_1,\dots,X_{64}$ be i.i.d. random variables with

$$
E[X_i]=12, \qquad \operatorname{Var}(X_i)=16.
$$

Define the sample mean

$$
M_{64}=\frac{X_1+\cdots+X_{64}}{64}.
$$

Task: Use Chebyshev's inequality to find an upper bound for $P(|M_{64}-12|\ge 1)$.

---

# Solution

First,

$$
E[M_{64}]=12,
\qquad
\operatorname{Var}(M_{64})=\frac{16}{64}=\frac{1}{4}.
$$

By Chebyshev's inequality,

$$
P(|M_{64}-12|\ge 1)
\le
\frac{\operatorname{Var}(M_{64})}{1^2}
=\frac{1}{4}.
$$

Hence,

$$
P(|M_{64}-12|\ge 1)\le 0.25.
$$
