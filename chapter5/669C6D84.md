# Problem 3 (Hard) — Polling with CLT

A polling company wants to estimate the fraction $p$ of voters who support a candidate.

They poll $n$ randomly selected voters and record the sample fraction $M_n$.

**Find the minimum sample size $n$ such that the polling error is within 0.05 of the true value $p$ with probability at least 0.96.**

That is, find the smallest $n$ satisfying:

$$P(|M_n - p| \geq 0.05) \leq 0.04$$

---

**Hint:** Use the CLT normal approximation with worst-case variance.

**Given:**
- $p(1-p) \leq 1/4$ for all $p$
- Worst-case standard deviation of $M_n - p$ is $1/(2\sqrt{n})$
- $\Phi(2.05) = 0.98$
