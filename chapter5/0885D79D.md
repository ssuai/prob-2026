# Problem 2 (Intermediate): Chebyshev Inequality + Weak Law of Large Numbers

## Problem Statement

A factory produces bolts whose diameters (in mm) are i.i.d. random variables $X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n$ with mean $\mu = 10$ mm and variance $\sigma^2 = 0.09$ mm².

Let $M_n = \dfrac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}$ be the sample mean.

**(a)** Compute $E[M_n]$ and $\text{var}(M_n)$.

**(b)** Using the **Chebyshev inequality**, find an upper bound for $P(|M_n - 10| \geq 0.1)$ when $n = 100$.

**(c)** Using the **Weak Law of Large Numbers**, what can you say about $P(|M_n - 10| \geq 0.1)$ as $n \to \infty$?

**(d)** Find the minimum sample size $n$ such that the Chebyshev bound guarantees
$$P(|M_n - 10| \geq 0.1) \leq 0.05.$$
