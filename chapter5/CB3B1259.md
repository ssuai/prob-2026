## Scenario

A factory produces bolts. The diameter $X_i$ of each bolt (in mm) is modeled as an i.i.d. random variable with **mean** $\mu = 4$ mm and **variance** $\sigma^2 = 9$ mm².

---

## Part A — Single Bolt, Chebyshev Inequality

The quality inspector wants to bound the probability that a single bolt's diameter deviates from the mean by at least 6 mm.

Using the **Chebyshev Inequality** (slide 10):

$$P(|X - \mu| \geq c) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{c^2}, \quad \text{for all } c > 0$$

1. Find an upper bound for $P(|X_1 - 4| \geq 6)$.

$$\text{(a)} \quad \underline{\hspace{4cm}}$$

---

## Part B — Sample Mean, Weak Law of Large Numbers

The inspector measures $n$ bolts and computes the sample mean:

$$M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}$$

The inspector wants to guarantee:

$$P(|M_n - 4| \geq 1) \leq \frac{1}{100}$$

Using the proof of the **Weak Law of Large Numbers** (slide 16), which gives:

$$P(|M_n - \mu| \geq \epsilon) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{n\,\epsilon^2}$$

2. Find the **minimum value of $n$** that guarantees the inspector's requirement.

$$\text{(b)} \quad \underline{\hspace{4cm}}$$

---

## Answer

**(a)**

$$P(|X_1 - 4| \geq 6) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{c^2} = \frac{9}{36} = \frac{1}{4}$$

**(b)** $n \geq 900$

---

## Explanation

**Step 1 — Part A.**  
Apply Chebyshev with $\mu = 4$, $\sigma^2 = 9$, and $c = 6$:

$$P(|X_1 - 4| \geq 6) \leq \frac{9}{6^2} = \frac{9}{36} = \frac{1}{4}$$

**Step 2 — Part B (setup).**  
From the WLLN proof on slide 16, $E[M_n] = \mu$ and $\text{var}(M_n) = \sigma^2/n$. Applying Chebyshev to $M_n$ with $\epsilon = 1$:

$$P(|M_n - 4| \geq 1) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{n \cdot \epsilon^2} = \frac{9}{n \cdot 1} = \frac{9}{n}$$

**Step 3 — Part B (solve).**  
Require $\dfrac{9}{n} \leq \dfrac{1}{100}$, which gives $n \geq 900$.

**Step 4 — Connection to WLLN.**  
This confirms the Weak Law of Large Numbers (slide 15): as $n \to \infty$, the bound $9/n \to 0$, so $P(|M_n - \mu| \geq \epsilon) \to 0$ for every $\epsilon > 0$.
