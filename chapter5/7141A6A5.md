# Intermediate Difficulty Problem: Estimating Total Delivery Time

A food delivery company wants to estimate the total delivery time for a large number of orders during a busy evening. Let \(X_i\) be the delivery time, measured in minutes, for the \(i\)-th order.

Assume that

\[
X_1, X_2, \dots, X_{64}
\]

are i.i.d. random variables with

\[
E[X_i]=30
\]

and

\[
SD(X_i)=8.
\]

The company is interested in the total delivery time

\[
S_{64}=X_1+X_2+\cdots+X_{64}.
\]

Although the individual delivery time \(X_i\) is not necessarily normally distributed, the company decides to use the Central Limit Theorem to approximate probabilities involving \(S_{64}\).

---

## Problem

**(a)** Define the standardized random variable \(Z_{64}\) corresponding to \(S_{64}\).

---

**(b)** Using the Central Limit Theorem, approximate

\[
P(S_{64}>2000).
\]

Express your answer using the standard normal CDF \(\Phi\), and then give a numerical approximation.

---

**(c)** Approximate the probability that the average delivery time of the \(64\) orders is greater than \(31.5\) minutes.

That is, approximate

\[
P\left(\frac{S_{64}}{64}>31.5\right).
\]

---

**(d)** The company wants the probability that the total delivery time exceeds a certain value \(T\) to be approximately \(0.05\). Find \(T\) such that

\[
P(S_{64}>T)\approx 0.05.
\]

---
