## Problem 2 (Intermediate)

A factory produces a large number of electronic components, and the lifespan of each component is independently and identically distributed with a mean of 50 months and a standard deviation of 8 months. Consider a random sample of 64 components, and let $\bar{X}$ be the sample mean of their lifespans. Determine a rigorous upper bound for the probability that the sample mean $\bar{X}$ deviates from the true population mean by $2$ months or more using Chebyshev's inequality, and subsequently calculate the approximate probability of this exact same event occurring by applying the Central Limit Theorem (expressing the CLT result using the standard normal CDF $\Phi$), in order to explicitly compare the theoretical looseness of the probability bound against the practical normal approximation.
