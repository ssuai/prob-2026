# Problem 2 (Intermediate) — Sample Mean and the Weak Law of Large Numbers

## Background

A quality control engineer at a factory wants to estimate the true defect rate $p$ of a production line. She repeatedly tests items independently; each test results in $X_i = 1$ (defective) or $X_i = 0$ (non-defective), where $P(X_i = 1) = p$. After $n$ tests, she computes the **sample mean** (empirical defect rate):

$$M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}$$

She wants $M_n$ to be close to the unknown true rate $p$.

---

## Questions

**(a)** Show that $\text{var}(M_n) \leq \dfrac{1}{4n}$.

> *Hint: For a Bernoulli$(p)$ random variable, $\text{var}(X_i) = p(1-p) \leq \dfrac{1}{4}$ for all $p \in [0,1]$.*

**(b)** Using **Chebyshev's inequality** and the result from **(a)**, derive an upper bound for $P(|M_n - p| \geq 0.05)$ in terms of $n$.

Then, find the **minimum sample size** $n$ such that:

$$P(|M_n - p| \geq 0.05) \leq 0.01$$

regardless of the true value of $p$.
