### [Medium Level] Question 2: IoT Sensor Network Accuracy (20 Points)

A smart city system monitors ambient temperatures using an array of $n$ independent IoT sensors. Each individual sensor's reading, $X_i$, is calibrated to have a true mean of $\mu = 25^\circ\text{C}$ and a variance of $\sigma^2 = 4$. To improve overall accuracy, the central system calculates the sample mean of the $n$ sensors, defined as $M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \dots + X_n}{n}$.

**(2-a)** [Chebyshev Bound on Sample Mean] Using Chebyshev's inequality applied to the sample mean, express the upper bound for the probability that the sample mean $M_n$ deviates from the true mean by $0.5^\circ\text{C}$ or more, $P(|M_n - 25| \ge 0.5)$, in terms of $n$.

**(2-b)** [Sample Size and WLLN] Calculate the absolute minimum number of sensors ($n$) required to guarantee that the probability of the sample mean deviating by $0.5^\circ\text{C}$ or more is at most $0.05$ ($5\%$). Explicitly explain how this result demonstrates the principle underlying the Weak Law of Large Numbers (WLLN).
