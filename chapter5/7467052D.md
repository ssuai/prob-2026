## Problem 2 (Intermediate)
An environmental lab estimates the average pollutant level by taking repeated independent measurements. Let $M_n=\frac{X_1+\cdots+X_n}{n}$ be the sample mean.
- The random variables $X_1,\ldots,X_n$ are **i.i.d.**
- Each measurement has mean $\mu=50$ and variance $\sigma^2=25$.
- Use **Chebyshev's inequality** for the sample mean: $P(|M_n-\mu|\ge \epsilon)\le \frac{\sigma^2}{n\epsilon^2}$.

Determine the **smallest integer sample size $n$** that guarantees $P(|M_n-50|<1.5)\ge 0.96$.
