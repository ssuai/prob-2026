We know the expected mean response time is μ=30 ms and the variance is σ^2=100 ms$^2$.

Using the Chebyshev inequality (which serves as the mathematical foundation for the Weak Law of Large Numbers), find the minimum number of queries n required to guarantee that the probability of the sample mean deviating from the true mean by 2 ms or more is at most 0.05.
