# Hard Problem 

A biased coin lands heads with probability \(0.6\). The coin is tossed repeatedly and independently.

Define

\[
X_i =
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if the \(i\)-th toss is heads}\\
0, & \text{if the \(i\)-th toss is tails}
\end{cases}
\]

and let

\[
M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}
\]

denote the empirical frequency of heads after \(n\) tosses.

1. Using the **Weak Law of Large Numbers**, explain what happens to

\[
P(|M_n - 0.6| \ge 0.05)
\]

as \(n \to \infty\).

2. Using the **Strong Law of Large Numbers**, explain the meaning of

\[
P\left(\lim_{n\to\infty} M_n = 0.6\right)=1
\]

in the context of repeated coin tosses.

3. A student claims:

> “The Weak Law and the Strong Law essentially give the same conclusion because both say that \(M_n\) approaches \(0.6\).”

Explain carefully why the Strong Law provides a stronger and more accurate statement than the Weak Law.
