## Problem 1: Bound Applications (Easy)

A non-negative continuous random variable $X$ representing the lifetime (in thousands of hours) of a specialized semiconductor component has a known expected value $E[X] = 4$. Through further testing, it is determined that its variance is $\text{var}(X) = 3$.

1. Using the Markov inequality, find an upper bound on the probability that a randomly chosen component lasts at least 10,000 hours (i.e., $P(X \ge 10)$).
2. Using the Chebyshev inequality, find an upper bound on the probability that the component's lifetime deviates from its expected value by 3,000 hours or more (i.e., $P(|X - 4| \ge 3)$).
