Intermediate: Tightening with Chebyshev
A semiconductor manufacturing process produces logic gates where the switching delay $X$ has a mean of $\mu = 120$ picoseconds (ps) and a variance of $\sigma^2 = 16$ ps$^2$.
Problem: You need to ensure the delay doesn't deviate too far from the average. Using Chebyshev's inequality, find the lower bound on the probability that a randomly selected logic gate will have a switching delay between 100 ps and 140 ps.
