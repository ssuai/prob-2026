## Problem 3 — Hard — CLT + Chebyshev Combined
A communication system sends bits $X_i \in \{0,1\}$ independently, where the probability of sending a 1 is $P(X_i = 1) = 0.6$. Define $S_n = X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n$ as the total number of 1s in $n$ transmissions.

**Questions:**

(a) Find the mean $E[S_n]$ and the variance $\text{Var}(S_n)$ in terms of $n$.

(b) For $n=100$, use the CLT to approximate $P(S_{100} \ge 70)$. (Use $\Phi(2.04) \approx 0.979$).

(c) Find the smallest value of $n$ such that:
$$P\left( \left| \frac{S_n}{n} - 0.6 \right| \le 0.02 \right) \ge 0.95$$
using the CLT. (Use $\Phi(1.96) \approx 0.975$).
