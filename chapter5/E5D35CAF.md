## Problem 3: CLT and Operational Risk (Hard)

An air cargo carrier loads $n = 100$ independent packages into a freight section. The weight of each package $X_i$ (for $i = 1, 2, \dots, 100$) is independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) following a continuous Uniform distribution between $10\text{ kg}$ and $70\text{ kg}$.

The maximum safe total weight capacity designated for this section is $4,200\text{ kg}$. 

1. Compute the theoretical mean $\mu = E[X_i]$ and variance $\sigma^2 = \text{var}(X_i)$ for a single package's weight.
2. Using the Central Limit Theorem (CLT), approximate the probability that the total weight of the 100 packages exceeds the maximum safe section capacity (i.e., $P(\sum_{i=1}^{100} X_i > 4200)$). Express your final answer using the standard normal CDF $\Phi(z)$ and round the numerical probability to four decimal places.
