## Problem 1 

A fair six-sided die is rolled $n$ times. Let $S_n$ denote the sum of the outcomes.

**(a)** Find $E[S_n]$ and $\text{var}(S_n)$.

**(b)** For $n = 120$, find an upper bound on $P(|S_{120} - E[S_{120}]| \geq 60)$ using the Chebyshev inequality.

**(c)** Find an upper bound on $P(S_n \geq 4n)$ using the Markov inequality. Is this bound dependent on $n$? Justify your answer.

**(d)** Using the Chebyshev inequality, find an upper bound on

$$P\!\left(\left|\frac{S_n}{n} - 3.5\right| \geq \epsilon\right)$$

and determine what happens to this bound as $n \to \infty$. What well-known theorem does this relate to?
