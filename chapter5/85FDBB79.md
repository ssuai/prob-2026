# Medium Problem: Weak Law of Large Numbers (WLLN)

A series of independent experiments \(X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n\) are conducted, where each random variable \(X_i\) has:

\[
\mu = 5
\]

and

\[
\sigma^2 = 10
\]

Let the sample mean be defined as:

\[
M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}
\]

Using the Chebyshev-based bound derived from the Weak Law of Large Numbers, determine how many experiments \(n\) must be conducted so that:

\[
P(|M_n - \mu| \ge 1) \le 0.05
\]
