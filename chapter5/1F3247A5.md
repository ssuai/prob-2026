# Intermidiate Problem

A factory produces light bulbs. Let $X_i$ be the lifetime, in hours, of the $i$-th randomly selected bulb. Assume that $X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n$ are i.i.d. (independent and identically distributed) random variables with:

$$E[X_i] = 1000, \qquad \operatorname{Var}(X_i) = 2500$$

Let $M_n$ be the sample mean lifetime:

$$M_n = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n}{n}$$

1. Find $E[M_n]$ and $\operatorname{Var}(M_n)$.
2. Using Chebyshev's inequality, find an upper bound for $P(|M_n - 1000| \ge 10)$ when $n = 100$.
3. Find the smallest $n$ such that Chebyshev's inequality guarantees:

$$P(|M_n - 1000| \ge 10) \le 0.01$$
