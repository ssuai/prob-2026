# Probability Problem Set: Difficulty 3 (advanced)

## Context: Fiber-Optic Communications (Joint PDF, Marginalization, Convolution)
In a fiber-optic communication system, a continuous signal amplitude $X$ is transmitted. Due to transmitter hardware limits, the transmitted signal $X$ is uniformly distributed over the interval $[0, A]$. During transmission, the physical channel corrupts the signal with additive noise $Z$. The noise $Z$ is exponentially distributed with rate parameter $\mu$ and is strictly non-negative ($Z \ge 0$). The noise is completely independent of the transmitted signal $X$. The receiver observes the continuous random variable $Y = X + Z$.

## Problem
Derive the marginal probability density function, $f_Y(y)$, for the received signal $Y$. Ensure you define the piecewise function for all possible values of $y$ ($-\infty < y < \infty$).
