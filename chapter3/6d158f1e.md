## Problem

### Korean

어느 콜센터에 걸려오는 전화 사이의 대기 시간 $T$는 평균이 $\frac{1}{\lambda} = 4$분인 지수분포를 따른다고 알려져 있다.

**(1)** $T$의 확률밀도함수(PDF) $f_T(t)$를 명시하고, 대기 시간이 2분 이하일 확률 $P(T \leq 2)$를 구하여라.

**(2)** 다음으로, 하루 동안 처리된 통화 수 $X$는 평균 $\mu = 50$, 표준편차 $\sigma = 5$인 정규분포를 따른다고 하자. 하루 처리 통화 수가 55건 이상 60건 이하일 확률 $P(55 \leq X \leq 60)$을 표준정규분포표를 이용하여 구하여라.

단, $\Phi(1) = 0.8413$, $\Phi(2) = 0.9772$ 로 주어진다. (여기서 $\Phi$는 표준정규분포의 CDF이다.)

---

### English

The waiting time $T$ between consecutive calls arriving at a call center is known to follow an exponential distribution with mean $\frac{1}{\lambda} = 4$ minutes.

**(1)** State the probability density function (PDF) $f_T(t)$ of $T$, and compute the probability that the waiting time is at most 2 minutes, $P(T \leq 2)$.

**(2)** Separately, let $X$ denote the number of calls handled in a single day. Suppose $X$ follows a normal distribution with mean $\mu = 50$ and standard deviation $\sigma = 5$. Using the standard normal table, compute $P(55 \leq X \leq 60)$.

You are given: $\Phi(1) = 0.8413$, $\Phi(2) = 0.9772$, where $\Phi$ denotes the CDF of the standard normal distribution.

---

## Solution

> **[Step 1] Model Setup**
>
> We define two separate probability models on the continuous sample space $\Omega = [0, \infty)$ (for $T$) and $\Omega = \mathbb{R}$ (for $X$).
>
> - $T \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda)$ with $\lambda = \frac{1}{4}$ (rate parameter), so the mean is $\frac{1}{\lambda} = 4$.
> - $X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2) = \mathcal{N}(50, 25)$.
>
> Both are continuous random variables; probabilities are computed by integrating the PDF over the relevant interval.

---

> **[Step 2] Key Observation**
>
> **Part (1) — Exponential CDF:**
> For $T \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda)$, the CDF has a closed form:
> $$F_T(t) = P(T \leq t) = 1 - e^{-\lambda t}, \quad t \geq 0.$$
> No integration is required once this formula is recognized.
>
> **Part (2) — Standardization of Normal:**
> Since $X \sim \mathcal{N}(50, 25)$, we standardize via
> $$Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma} = \frac{X - 50}{5} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1).$$
> Then $P(55 \leq X \leq 60) = P\!\left(\frac{55-50}{5} \leq Z \leq \frac{60-50}{5}\right) = P(1 \leq Z \leq 2).$

---

> **[Step 3] Derivation**
>
> **Part (1):**
>
> The PDF of $T$ is
> $$f_T(t) = \lambda e^{-\lambda t} = \frac{1}{4}e^{-t/4}, \quad t \geq 0,$$
> and $f_T(t) = 0$ for $t < 0$.
>
> Therefore,
> $$P(T \leq 2) = \int_0^{2} \frac{1}{4} e^{-t/4}\, dt = \left[-e^{-t/4}\right]_0^{2} = 1 - e^{-1/2}.$$
>
> **Part (2):**
>
> $$P(55 \leq X \leq 60) = P(1 \leq Z \leq 2) = \Phi(2) - \Phi(1).$$

---

> **[Step 4] Computation**
>
> **Part (1):**
> $$P(T \leq 2) = 1 - e^{-0.5} \approx 1 - 0.6065 = 0.3935.$$
>
> **Part (2):**
> $$P(55 \leq X \leq 60) = \Phi(2) - \Phi(1) = 0.9772 - 0.8413 = 0.1359.$$

---

> **[Step 5] Verification**
>
> - **Part (1):** $0 \leq 0.3935 \leq 1$. (OK)
>   As $t \to \infty$, $P(T \leq t) \to 1$, consistent with a proper CDF. (OK)
>
> - **Part (2):** $0 \leq 0.1359 \leq 1$. (OK)
>   By symmetry of $\mathcal{N}(0,1)$, the interval $[1,2]$ lies in the right tail beyond one standard deviation, so a probability near $0.14$ is geometrically plausible. (OK)

---

**Answer:**

$$P(T \leq 2) = 1 - e^{-1/2} \approx 0.39, \qquad P(55 \leq X \leq 60) = 0.1359.$$
