\## 3. Hard Level

\*\*Topic: Normal Distribution and Signal Detection Error\*\*

\*\*\[Problem]\*\*

In a digital communication system, a binary message $X$ is sent as either $-1$ or $1$ with equal probability.\[cite\_start]The received signal $Y$ is the transmitted signal $X$ plus noise $Z$ ($Y = X + Z$).The noise $Z$ follows a normal distribution with a mean of 0 and a variance of 4 ($Z \\sim N(0, 4)$).

The receiver decides that $1$ was sent if $Y \\ge 0$. Find the probability that the receiver mistakenly decides $1$ was sent when the actual transmitted message was $-1$. \[cite\_start]Express your answer using the standard normal cumulative distribution function $\\Phi(z)$.
