Problem 3 — Hard Level: Normal Distribution, Conditional Independence, and Hidden Variable Reasoning

Problem Statement

Let Z\sim N(0,1), and let \varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2\sim N(0,1) be independent of each other and independent of Z.

Define

X = Z+\varepsilon_1,\qquad Y=Z+\varepsilon_2
	1.	Show that X and Y are not independent.
	2.	Show that X and Y are conditionally independent given Z.

Given Information / Assumptions
	•	All random variables are jointly continuous.
	•	Sum of independent normal random variables is normal.
	•	If two random variables are independent, then their covariance is zero (when finite).
