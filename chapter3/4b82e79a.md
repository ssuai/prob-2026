### [Hard Level] Question 3: Binary Signal Detection with Gaussian Noise (25 Points)

In a digital communication system, a transmitter sends a discrete binary signal $X$ over a noisy channel. 
* The signal is $X = 1$ with probability $p$.
* The signal is $X = -1$ with probability $1 - p$.

The receiver picks up a continuous observation $Y$, which is the original signal corrupted by additive channel noise $W$, such that:
$$Y = X + W$$

The noise $W$ follows a Standard Normal (Gaussian) distribution, $W \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$. Because the noise is normally distributed, the conditional PDF of the received signal $Y$ given the transmitted signal $X$, denoted as $f_{Y|X}(y|x)$, is a normal distribution centered at $x$ with a variance of $1$.

**(3-a)** [Bayes' Rule] Suppose the receiver makes a specific continuous observation $Y = y$. Write down the explicit mathematical equation for the conditional probability $P(X = 1 | Y = y)$. You must use the continuous/discrete mixed version of Bayes' rule. Write out the full formula including the prior probabilities and the conditional PDFs $f_{Y|X}(y|1)$ and $f_{Y|X}(y|-1)$.

**(3-b)** [Simplification] Substitute the actual normal density functions into your Bayes' rule equation from (3-a). Perform the algebraic steps necessary to simplify the expression as much as possible. Your final answer for $P(X = 1 | Y = y)$ must be expressed strictly in terms of the prior probability $p$, the observed value $y$, and the exponential function $e$. All square roots and $\pi$ terms must be algebraically canceled out.
