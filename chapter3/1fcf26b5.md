## Problem

### Korean

어떤 기계 부품의 수명(단위: 년) $X$는 다음의 누적분포함수(CDF)를 따른다고 하자.

$$F_X(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & x < 0 \\ 1 - e^{-x^2/2} & x \geq 0 \end{cases}$$

**(1)** $X$의 확률밀도함수(PDF) $f_X(x)$를 구하고, 이것이 유효한 PDF임을 확인하여라.

**(2)** $P(1 \leq X \leq 2)$를 구하여라.

**(3)** $X$의 기댓값 $E[X]$를 구하여라.
(힌트: $\int_0^\infty x^2 e^{-x^2/2}\, dx = \dfrac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{2}$ 임을 이용하여라.)

**(4)** 새로운 확률변수 $Y = X^2$을 정의하자. $Y$의 CDF $F_Y(y)$와 PDF $f_Y(y)$를 구하여라.

**(5)** (4)에서 구한 $f_Y(y)$가 어떤 표준 분포 계열에 속하는지 확인하고, 그 분포의 이름과 모수를 명시하여라.

---

### English

The lifetime (in years) $X$ of a mechanical component has the cumulative distribution function (CDF):

$$F_X(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & x < 0 \\ 1 - e^{-x^2/2} & x \geq 0 \end{cases}$$

**(1)** Derive the PDF $f_X(x)$ and verify that it is a valid PDF.

**(2)** Compute $P(1 \leq X \leq 2)$.

**(3)** Find the expected value $E[X]$.
(Hint: You may use $\int_0^\infty x^2 e^{-x^2/2}\, dx = \dfrac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{2}$.)

**(4)** Define a new random variable $Y = X^2$. Derive the CDF $F_Y(y)$ and the PDF $f_Y(y)$.

**(5)** Identify which standard distribution family $f_Y(y)$ belongs to (from part (4)), and state its name and parameters.

---

## Solution

> **[Step 1] Model Setup**
>
> The sample space is $\Omega = [0, \infty)$, representing non-negative lifetimes.
>
> $X$ is a **continuous** random variable with CDF $F_X(x) = 1 - e^{-x^2/2}$ for $x \geq 0$.
> Since $F_X$ is differentiable on $(0,\infty)$, the PDF is obtained by differentiation:
> $$f_X(x) = \frac{d}{dx} F_X(x), \quad x > 0.$$
>
> For part (4), $Y = g(X) = X^2$ is a strictly increasing, differentiable function on $(0,\infty)$, so the derived distribution can be found via the **CDF method**:
> $$F_Y(y) = P(Y \leq y) = P(X^2 \leq y) = P(X \leq \sqrt{y}) = F_X(\sqrt{y}).$$

---

> **[Step 2] Key Observation / Diagram**
>
> The shape of $F_X$ resembles a Weibull-type CDF: it starts at 0, is strictly increasing for $x > 0$, and approaches 1 as $x \to \infty$.
>
> ```
>   F_X(x)
>   1.0 |                          .........
>       |                   ......
>       |             .....
>   0.5 |         ....
>       |      ...
>       |   ..
>   0.0 +--+-------+-------+----> x
>       0  0.5     1       2
> ```
>
> Key observations:
> - $F_X(0) = 1 - e^0 = 0.$ (OK — CDF starts at 0)
> - $\lim_{x\to\infty} F_X(x) = 1.$ (OK — proper CDF)
> - $F_X$ is non-decreasing and right-continuous. (OK)
>
> For part (5): the PDF of $Y$ will be matched against the **Exponential** family $f(y) = \lambda e^{-\lambda y}$, $y \geq 0$.

---

> **[Step 3] Derivation**
>
> **Part (1) — PDF of $X$:**
>
> Differentiating $F_X(x) = 1 - e^{-x^2/2}$:
> $$f_X(x) = \frac{d}{dx}\!\left(1 - e^{-x^2/2}\right) = e^{-x^2/2} \cdot x = x\, e^{-x^2/2}, \quad x \geq 0.$$
>
> **Validity check:**
>
> (i) $f_X(x) = x e^{-x^2/2} \geq 0$ for all $x \geq 0$. (OK)
>
> (ii) Normalization — using the substitution $u = x^2/2$, $du = x\, dx$:
> $$\int_0^\infty x\, e^{-x^2/2}\, dx = \int_0^\infty e^{-u}\, du = \left[-e^{-u}\right]_0^\infty = 1. \quad \text{(OK)}$$
>
> ---
>
> **Part (2) — $P(1 \leq X \leq 2)$:**
>
> $$P(1 \leq X \leq 2) = F_X(2) - F_X(1) = \left(1 - e^{-2}\right) - \left(1 - e^{-1/2}\right) = e^{-1/2} - e^{-2}.$$
>
> ---
>
> **Part (3) — $E[X]$:**
>
> $$E[X] = \int_0^\infty x \cdot f_X(x)\, dx = \int_0^\infty x \cdot x\, e^{-x^2/2}\, dx = \int_0^\infty x^2\, e^{-x^2/2}\, dx.$$
>
> Applying the given hint:
> $$E[X] = \frac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{2}.$$
>
> ---
>
> **Part (4) — Distribution of $Y = X^2$:**
>
> For $y \geq 0$:
> $$F_Y(y) = P(Y \leq y) = P(X^2 \leq y) = P(X \leq \sqrt{y}) = F_X(\!\sqrt{y}) = 1 - e^{-y/2}.$$
>
> ($X \geq 0$ so $|X| = X$; no need to consider $X \leq -\sqrt{y}$.)
>
> Differentiating to get the PDF:
> $$f_Y(y) = \frac{d}{dy}\!\left(1 - e^{-y/2}\right) = \frac{1}{2}\, e^{-y/2}, \quad y \geq 0.$$
>
> ---
>
> **Part (5) — Identification of distribution:**
>
> Comparing with the general Exponential PDF $f(y) = \lambda\, e^{-\lambda y}$ ($y \geq 0$), we identify:
> $$\lambda = \frac{1}{2}.$$
>
> Therefore, $Y = X^2 \sim \text{Exponential}\!\left(\lambda = \tfrac{1}{2}\right)$, with mean $E[Y] = \dfrac{1}{\lambda} = 2$.

---

> **[Step 4] Computation**
>
> | Part | Result |
> |------|--------|
> | (1) | $f_X(x) = x\,e^{-x^2/2},\quad x \geq 0$ |
> | (2) | $P(1 \leq X \leq 2) = e^{-1/2} - e^{-2} \approx 0.6065 - 0.1353 = 0.4712$ |
> | (3) | $E[X] = \dfrac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{2} \approx \dfrac{2.5066}{2} \approx 1.25$ |
> | (4) | $F_Y(y) = 1 - e^{-y/2}$, $\quad f_Y(y) = \tfrac{1}{2}e^{-y/2},\quad y \geq 0$ |
> | (5) | $Y \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda = \tfrac{1}{2})$ |

---

> **[Step 5] Verification**
>
> - **$f_X$ integrates to 1:** shown in Step 3, Part (1). (OK)
>
> - **$P(1 \leq X \leq 2) \in [0,1]$:** $e^{-1/2} \approx 0.607 > e^{-2} \approx 0.135$, so the result is positive. (OK)
>
> - **$E[X] > 0$:** consistent with $X \geq 0$ taking positive values. (OK)
>
> - **$f_Y$ normalization:**
>   $$\int_0^\infty \frac{1}{2} e^{-y/2}\, dy = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 = 1. \quad \text{(OK)}$$
>
> - **$E[Y] = E[X^2]$ consistency:**
>   Since $Y \sim \text{Exp}(\tfrac{1}{2})$, $E[Y] = 2$. Alternatively, $E[X^2] = \text{Var}(X) + (E[X])^2$. A direct check via integration confirms this is consistent. (OK)
>
> - **Modeling trap — common error:** Students might incorrectly write $F_Y(y) = P(-\sqrt{y} \leq X \leq \sqrt{y})$ as for a symmetric distribution. Here $X \geq 0$, so only the upper bound $X \leq \sqrt{y}$ applies. (OK)

---

**Answer:**

$$f_X(x) = x\,e^{-x^2/2} \;(x \geq 0), \qquad P(1 \leq X \leq 2) = e^{-1/2} - e^{-2} \approx 0.47,$$

$$E[X] = \frac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{2} \approx 1.25,$$

$$f_Y(y) = \frac{1}{2}e^{-y/2} \;(y \geq 0), \qquad Y = X^2 \sim \text{Exponential}\!\left(\lambda = \tfrac{1}{2}\right).$$
