## Problem 2: Obstacle Detection in Autonomous AI

**[Core Concept: Normal Distribution and Interval Probability]**

**[Problem Scenario]**
An autonomous vehicle's LiDAR sensor measures the distance to an obstacle as 10m. 

However, the measurement has a Gaussian noise $Z$ with a mean of 0 and a variance of 4. 

This means the actual observed distance $Y$ follows a normal distribution: $Y \sim \mathcal{N}(10, 4)$. 

Calculate the probability $P(9 \le Y \le 12)$ that the observed distance falls between 9m and 12m.

(Note: Use $\Phi(1.0) = 0.8413$, $\Phi(0.5) = 0.6915$)
