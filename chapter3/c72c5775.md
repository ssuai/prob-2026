### Problem - Hard

A communication system transmits signal $s \in \{-1, +1\}$ with equal probability. The received signal is $R = s + N$, where $N \sim N(0, \sigma^2)$ is noise. The receiver decides $\hat{s} = +1$ if $R \ge 0$, and $\hat{s} = -1$ if $R < 0$.
(a) Find the probability of error $P(\hat{s} \neq s)$ in terms of $\Phi$ and $\sigma$.
(b) For what value of $\sigma$ is $P(\text{error}) = 0.1587$? (Use $\Phi(1) = 0.8413$.)
(c) If we change the decision threshold to $\tau$ (decide $+1$ if $R \ge \tau$), find the optimal $\tau$ that minimizes $P(\text{error})$ when the two signals are transmitted with equal probability. Justify your answer.
