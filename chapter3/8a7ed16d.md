Problem 1 — Easy Level: Conditional Independence in Medical Screening

Problem Statement

A population is tested for a rare condition using two screening devices, Device A and Device B. Let:
	•	D: event that a randomly selected person has the condition
	•	A: event that Device A gives a positive result
	•	B: event that Device B gives a positive result

Suppose:
	•	P(D)=0.1
	•	P(A \mid D)=0.9,\quad P(A \mid D^c)=0.2
	•	P(B \mid D)=0.8,\quad P(B \mid D^c)=0.1

Assume that given whether or not the person has the condition, the test outcomes of Device A and Device B are conditionally independent.

Determine whether events A and B are independent without conditioning. Justify your answer mathematically.

Given Information / Assumptions
	•	A \perp B \mid D
	•	A \perp B \mid D^c
