**Question 6: Signal Detection, Normal Noise, and Posterior Inference**
This problem synthesizes the exponential distribution, normal random variables, and continuous Bayes' theorem. 
Suppose a transmitted optical signal has an intensity $T$, modeled as an exponential random variable with parameter $lambda = 1$. The signal is transmitted over a noisy channel. The received signal is $X = T + W$, where $W$ is standard normal noise ($N(0,1)$), independent of $T$.
- **(a)** Given that $T = t$, state the conditional PDF $f_{X|T}(x|t)$.
- **(b)** Using the continuous form of Bayes' rule, derive the posterior PDF $f_{T|X}(t|x)$ for $t > 0$. Simplify the exponent to show it takes the form of a truncated normal distribution. 
- **(c)** To find the Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimate of the signal, we need to maximize the posterior PDF. Given an observation $X = x$, find the value of $t > 0$ that maximizes $f_{T|X}(t|x)$. 
- **(d)** Challenge: Set up the integral for the marginal PDF of the received signal, $f_X(x)$, and express it in terms of the standard normal CDF, $Phi$.
