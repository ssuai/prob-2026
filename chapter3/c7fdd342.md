# Problem 3: Autonomous LiDAR Sensor Calibration

**Problem Statement:**
An autonomous vehicle uses a LiDAR sensor to detect obstacles in its path. The true distance to the nearest obstacle ahead, denoted by the continuous random variable $X$, follows an Exponential distribution with a rate parameter $\lambda > 0$.
Therefore, $f_X(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}$ for $x \ge 0$ (and $0$ otherwise).

Due to hardware imperfections and environmental noise, the sensor's measurement is not perfectly accurate. When the true distance is $X = x$, the measurement $Y$ is subject to zero-mean Gaussian noise with variance $\sigma^2$. Thus, the conditional distribution of $Y$ given $X = x$ is Normal:
$$Y|X = x \sim \mathcal{N}(x, \sigma^2)$$

**Your Task:**
1. **Part A:** Write the joint probability density function $f_{X,Y}(x, y)$ for $x \ge 0$ and $-\infty < y < \infty$.
2. **Part B:** Derive the marginal probability density function of the sensor measurement, $f_Y(y)$. Express your final answer using $\Phi(\cdot)$, which represents the cumulative distribution function (CDF) of the Standard Normal distribution.
