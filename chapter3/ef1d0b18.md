## Problem

A factory produces metal rods whose length $X$ (in cm) follows a normal distribution:

$$
X \sim \mathcal{N}(100, 4^2)
$$

The rods are classified into three types based on their length:

- **Type A**: $X \ge 102$
- **Type B**: $98 \le X < 102$
- **Type C**: $X < 98$

The lifetime $Y$ (in years) of a rod depends on its type and follows an exponential distribution:

- Type A: $Y \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda = 0.15)$
- Type B: $Y \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda = 0.3)$
- Type C: $Y \sim \text{Exponential}(\lambda = 0.6)$

A randomly selected rod is observed to have a lifetime of $Y = 2$ years.

Answer the following:

1. Compute $P(\text{Type A}),\; P(\text{Type B}),\; P(\text{Type C})$.
2. Derive the overall probability density function $f_Y(y)$ in mixture form.
3. Compute $P(Y \le 2)$.
4. Using Bayes' rule, compute $P(\text{Type A} \mid Y = 2)$.
