## Problem 1: Lifespan of Smart Components (Easy)

The product testing division of an electronics company is evaluating a new continuous random variable $X$, which represents the lifespan (measured in thousands of hours) of a smart sensor. Analysis shows that $X$ conforms to a Normal Distribution with a mean of $\mu = 8.5$ (i.e., 8,500 hours) and a standard deviation of $\sigma = 1.2$.

**Questions:**

**(a)** Formulate the statistical expression to find the probability that a randomly selected smart sensor will last between 7,300 hours and 10,900 hours. Convert these bounds to standard normal $Z$-scores.

**(b)** The company wants to establish a "premium guarantee" marketing campaign. If they want to guarantee a lifespan such that only the bottom $5\%$ of the sensors fail before the guaranteed time, how many hours should this premium warranty cover? *(Assume you have access to a standard normal $Z$-table).*
