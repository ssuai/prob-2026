# Problem 2 (Intermediate)

Let $X_1, X_2, X_3$ be independent random variables, each uniformly distributed on the interval $[0,1]$.

Define a new random variable

$$
X = \max\{X_1, X_2, X_3\}.
$$

Task: Find the probability density function (PDF) of $X$.

---

# Solution

We first find the CDF of $X$.

$$
F_X(x)=P(X\le x)
$$

Since $X=\max\{X_1,X_2,X_3\}$,

$$
F_X(x)=P(X_1\le x,\ X_2\le x,\ X_3\le x)
$$

Because $X_1, X_2, X_3$ are independent,

$$
F_X(x)=P(X_1\le x)P(X_2\le x)P(X_3\le x)
$$

Each $X_i$ is uniform on $[0,1]$, so for $0\le x\le 1$,

$$
P(X_i\le x)=x
$$

Thus,

$$
F_X(x)=x^3, \qquad 0\le x\le 1
$$

Now differentiate to get the PDF:

$$
f_X(x)=\frac{d}{dx}F_X(x)=3x^2, \qquad 0\le x\le 1
$$

Therefore,

$$
f_X(x)=
\begin{cases}
3x^2, & 0\le x\le 1 \\
0, & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
