## Problem 3: Multi-stage AI Inference Latency Analysis

**[Core Concept: Exponential and Normal Distribution Joint Model]**

**[Problem Scenario]**
An AI server processes a task in two stages. The processing time of the first stage, $X$, follows an **exponential distribution** with a mean of 10ms. 

The additional time required for the second stage, $Z$, follows a **normal distribution** with a mean of 0 and a variance that depends on the first stage: $Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, X^2/100)$. 

The total inference time is defined as $Y = X + Z$. Given that the first stage took exactly $x = 20ms$, calculate the probability $P(Y \le 22 | X = 20)$ that the total inference time is 22ms or less.
